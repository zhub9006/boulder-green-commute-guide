## 🌡️ High-Altitude (5,430 ft) Special Considerations

Boulder sits at **5,430 ft elevation** (1,655 m) — here's what that means:

### Physiological Effects

| Symptom | Walking Impact | Biking Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lower O₂** (≈17% less than sea level) | Get tired 10–15% faster | Get tired 10–15% faster | Give yourself 3–5 days to acclimatize before long commutes |
| **UV exposure** (≈15–20% stronger per 1,000 ft) | Sunburn faster — SPF 30+ minimum | Same! Sunburn in 10 min on a clear day | Wear long sleeves; SPF 50+; lip balm with SPF |
| **Low humidity** (~30% avg) |Dehydration faster — drink more | Same! Breathing dry air loses more water | Drink 0.5L per 30 min; use saline nasal spray |
| **Rapid weather changes** | Afternoon storms especially in spring/summer | Same wind/rain risk; +10 mph gusts possible | Always carry a rain jacket; check weather before leaving |
| **Altitude heart rate** | HR 10–15 bpm higher at same speed | HR same 10–15 bpm higher | Don't be alarmed; your body adjusts in 3–5 days |

### ☀️ UV At-Altitude Calculator

Boulder's UV index is roughly `sea-level UV index × (5,430 ÷ 1,000 × 0.15) ≈ × 1.17` stronger. So if the Denver UV index is 8, Boulder's ≈ 9.4. At 11 AM in July, Boulder can reach PPE UV index of 10–11 (extreme)!

## 🎯 Boulder Green Commuting "Quick Tips" — The 10 Commandments

### 🚶 Walking

1. **☀️ Always apply SPF 30+ at altitude** — your skin burns 15–20% faster in Boulder at 5,430 ft
2. **🚲 Ride your bike to work after you walk it** — both use the same route; walking iteration helps you plan your safest biking line
3. **🚶‍♀️ Walk facing traffic on 20th Street** — no bike lane; residential street allows two-way traffic
4. **⏱️ Window on 19th Street** — the wait signals at Euclid & 21st take 20–60 seconds each; plan around them
5. **✨ Canyon Blvd is a hidden gem** — slow down on this section; the Flatirons views are your reward for making it to downtown Boulder!

### 🚲 Biking

1. **🚲 Always, ALWAYS, take the FULL 19th St bike lane** — dooring zone risk means never ride near parked cars; don't try to "share" in the bike lane and car door zone
2. **🚲 Basic right hook rule** — if a car ahead of you is turning right, take the lane before the intersection to signal your presence
3. **🚲 ALWAYS use hand signals** — Colorado law requires left arm extended horizontally for left turns, and right arm extended horizontally for right turns
4. **🚲 PEARL STREET = 🚫 DISMOUNT ZONE** — The pedestrian mall section requires walking your bike; Boulder Municipal Code prohibits biking through the mall
5. **🚲 Canyon Blvd is Separation Heaven** — at 637 m with a physical barrier separating you from cars, this is literally Boulder's best bike infrastructure — enjoy the ride!

### ⚡ General

6. **🚲 If weather is bad, take RTD bus** — 1/3/7/10 runs every 15 min on 19th St + bike rack on front of every RTD bus
7. **🛴 Consider B-Cycle** — $99/yr annual pass covers the 16-minute trip (free!); let B-Cycle worry about bike maintenance; always dock at one of the 2 nearby stations
8. **🌲 Off-peak = better experience** — 19th Street from 9:30 AM to 3:30 PM and after 6:30 PM is always less busy; that's the sweet spot
9. **🚶 Walk your bike on 19th Street** — if you're feeling nervous, just walk your bike on the sidewalk on 19th Street — it takes ~48 minutes exactly
10. **🌍 Every active trip counts** — If you walk or bike instead of driving, that's **0.8 kg CO₂ saved** per trip (that's 200 kg CO₂ saved per year at 250 commuting days!)
