# 💡 Green Commuting Tips & Seasonal Guide

---

## 🌸 Spring (March–May)

### Walking
- **Weather:** Mild, 50-65°F; occasional rain showers
- **Gear:** Light waterproof jacket; umbrella; trail runners or walking shoes
- **Bonus:** Wildflowers bloom along Boulder Canyon; Canyon daisies and pasqueflowers
- **Tip:** Rain on 19th St? Take 20th St/Bradford as alternate connector

### Biking
- **Weather:** 45-65°F; occasional wind
- **Gear:** Windbreaker, cycling gloves, fenders for spring puddles
- **Tip:** Spring melt can cause muddy sections on multi-use paths; avoid off-road detours

---

## ☀️ Summer (June–August)

### Walking
- **Weather:** 75-95°F; very dry at altitude (5,430 ft)
- **Hydration:** **CRITICAL** — carry 1-2 liters; dehydration happens faster at altitude
- **Sun:** Apply SPF 30+ before walking; wear a hat; UV rays are 25% stronger at Boulder's altitude
- **Trail Tip:** Walk Canyon Blvd in the morning (cooler); the path's tree shade helps
- **Evening:** Sunset at ~8:15 PM; great for evening walks with longer daylight

### Biking
- **Weather:** 70-95°F; Tucson-bordering-arid air
- **Hydration:** Carry at least 1 liter; consider a bottle cage or hydration pack
- **Gear:** Lightweight sun jersey; sunglasses; SPF lip balm
- **Tip:** 19th St gets hot pavement — watch for soft asphalt in extreme heat
- **Bonus:** Free Splash pads for cooling off near Pearl Street area

---

## 🍂 Fall (September–November)

### Walking
- **Weather:** 45-68°F; gorgeous fall foliage
- **Gear:** Layered clothing; light gloves; hat for chilly mornings
- **Bonus:** Aspen leaves turn gold along Canyon Blvd — Colorado's best fall color viewing!
- **Tip:** Fall is the PERFECT season for walking commutes — cool temps, beautiful scenery
- **School:** RTD routes may have more students in fall; watch for pedestrians

### Biking
- **Weather:** 40-65°F; typically Boulder's best biking weather
- **Gear:** Full-finger gloves; leg warmers; light layers
- **Tip:** Fall sun position is lower — sunglasses essential for glare on 19th St
- **Bonus:** Fewer bugs; dry roads until late November; perfect for B-Cycle rides

---

## ❄️ Winter (December–February)

### Walking
- **Weather:** 20-45°F; snow/ice; shorter daylight (~5:45 PM sunset)
- **Traction:** Wear Microspikes or Yaktrax over shoes — icy sidewalks are a real hazard
- **Visibility:** Wear reflective clothing/vest; use a headlamp (Boulder's dawn and dusk are early!
- **Gear:** Insulated waterproof boots; wool socks; down or synthetic puffy jacket
- **Warm-Up:** Walk 19th St — the RTD buses warm the pavement slightly, giving better traction
- **Tip:** On icy days, consider B-Cycle (they're often plowed faster) or bus + walk

### Biking
- **Weather:** 15-40°F; snow/ice possible; wind chill drops temps further
- **Tires:** **Studded tires** are a must for ice; regular tires = dangerous
- **Visibility:** Front + rear lights (daytime too); reflective ankle bands (moving part = more visible)
- **Route:** Consider the **Boulder Creek Path** alternative — the separator bike lane on Canyon Blvd is often plowed/cleared before 19th St
- **Safety:** If 19th St is icy, take Bradford to 15th to avoid the arterial
- **Tip:** Best winter biking is on clear, cold days (below 32°F); wet snow at 33°F = black ice

## ⚡ Quick-Prep Checklists

### 🚶 Walking Kit (Keep in Your Bag)
- ✅ Reusable water bottle (Boulder's altitude dehydrates you fast)
- ✅ SPF 30+ lip balm + face sunscreen
- ✅ Light rain jacket or compact umbrella
- ✅ Headlamp or small flashlight (for darker winter months)
- ✅ Reflective armband or vest (for winter cycling/biking too)
- ✅ A pair of comfortable insoles — your feet will thank you after 48 min!

### 🚴 Biking Kit (Keep in Your Bag)
- ✅ U-lock (maximum security for 1800 Broadway; cable + U-lock combo)
- ✅ Front + rear lights (USB-rechargeable recommended)
- ✅ Compact multitool + tire lever + mini pump
- ✅ Fenders (for 19th St splashes; winter mud)
- ✅ Helmet (required under 18; recommended always)
- ✅ Sunglasses or cycling glasses (UV + wind + bugs)
- ✅ Small first-aid kit (band-aid, blister pads, ibuprofen)
- ✅ Phone mount + charger (navigation/backup)

## 🧗🏔️ Boulder-Specific Altitude Tips

### Why Altitude Matters
Boulder sits at **5,430 ft (1,655 m)** — that's higher than most commercial airplane cabins!

| Symptom | How It Affects Your Commute | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Dehydration | You lose water faster | Drink 500 mL extra per hour of walking/biking |
| UV exposure | Sunburn in 15 min (not 30) at mall elevations | SPF 30+ minimum; reapply if walking >30 min |
| Shortness of breath | Walking/biking feels harder initially | Start slow; your body adapts in 1-2 weeks |
| Fatigue | Quicker tiredness | Allow 1 week adjustment period for new routine |
| Temperature swings | Rapid drops in evening | Always carry extra layer even in summer! |

## 🔁 Mixing Modes: The Ultimate Flexible Green Commute

### Option 1: Walk-to-Bike Rotation
| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Walk | Bike | Walk | Bike | Walk |

- Balance exercise and time
- Protects from overuse injuries
- Fits weather variations

### Option 2: Bike + Bus Combo
- Bike to 19th Street → Catch RTD Bus → Disembark near Broadway
- Great on rainy/windy days or when you're running late
- RTD route 1, 3, 7, 10 all cover 19th St

### Option 3: One-Way Walk, One-Way Bike
- **Morning:** Bike to work (16 min, arrive fresh)
- **Evening:** Walk home (48 min, unwind with Creek views)
- **Why it works:** Walking home can be meditative after a day at work; biking morning gets you there fast

### Option 4: Walk One Way, Bus/RTD Other
- Walk or bike one direction, take RTD other
- Perfect if you vary your schedule
