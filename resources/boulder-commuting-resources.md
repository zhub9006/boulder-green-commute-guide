# 🗺️ Boulder Green Commuting Resources

## Bike Infrastructure

- **Boulder Creek Path** — Paved, car-free multi-use trail along Boulder Creek. Connects Canyon Blvd to downtown.
- **B-Cycle Boulder** — Public bike-share system. Stations near Pearl Street, CU Boulder, downtown.
- **19th Street bike lanes** — Dedicated bike lanes on 19th Street southbound
- **Canyon Boulevard** — Part of the regional bike network

## Transit Options

- **RTD Bus** — Regional Transportation District runs buses through Boulder. Bikes allowed on buses.
- **Slack Mobility** — On-demand shuttle service in Boulder
- **U-Pass** — CU Boulder students get free RTD access

## Walking Routes

- **Pearl Street Mall** — Car-free pedestrian downtown shopping district
- **Chautauqua Park** — Walking trails with mountain views (west of downtown)
- **Boulder Creek Path** — Scenic walking/biking along the creek

## Weather & Seasonal Tips

- **Sunshine:** ~300 days/year — sunscreen and hydration essential
- **Temperature swings:** Can vary 20°F+ in a single day; dress in layers
- **Rain:** Most common in spring and summer thunderstorms; carry a light packable rain jacket
- **Winter:** Snow can make routes icy; use studded bike tires in winter
- **Wind:** Boulder is windy; biking with a tailwind can save 5+ minutes

## Safety Tips

- Always use hand signals when biking
- Wear a helmet (Boulder city code for cyclists under 18)
- Use lights at dawn/dusk
- Be visible — bright clothing even in daylight
- Watch for bike joke tellers (traffic-calming devices) in neighborhoods
