## Detailed Step-by-Step Directions

### Segment 1: Oak Avenue (96.6 m)
- **Duration:** ~30 seconds
- **Description:** Start heading south on Oak Avenue from your home
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Shared road, low speed limit
- **Notes:** Residential street, very bike-friendly

### Segment 2: 19th Street (2.16 km)
- **Duration:** ~3-4 minutes
- **Description:** Continue south on 19th Street — the main commuting segment
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Dedicated bike lane
- **Notes:** 19th Street is one of Boulder's primary north-south bike corridors. The bike lane is well-maintained and separates cyclists from vehicle traffic.

### Segment 3: 20th St. (106 m)
- **Duration:** ~30 seconds
- **Description:** Navigate through the 20th St. intersection

- **Notes:** Yield to pedestrians at crosswalks

### Segment 4: 20th Street (566 m)
- **Duration:** ~1 minute

### Segment 5: Pearl Street (216 m)

- **Description:** Continue to/East on Pearl Street
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Shared road, slow traffic
- **Notes:** Pearl Street in downtown Boulder has heavy pedestrian traffic — slow down and be prepared to stop

### Segment 6: 18th Street (249 m)
- **Duration:** ~1 minute
- **Description:** Continue south on 18th Street
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Shared road
- **Notes:** Short segment leading to Canyon Boulevard

### Segment 7: Canyon Boulevard (637 m)

## Bike Maintenance Tips for Boulder Commuters

- **Tire Pressure:** Check weekly — Boulder's altitude means slightly lower air density
- **Brake Pads:** Inspect monthly; dust from dry climates can wear pads faster
- **Chain Lubrication:** Lubricate every 2 weeks or after wet rides
- **Winter Riding:** Use studded tires December-February if icy conditions are present
- **Theft Prevention:** Always lock your bike; use a U-lock + cable for maximum security
