## Step-by-Step Directions

### Leg 1: Oak Avenue (96.6 m, ~2 min)
Head south on **Oak Avenue** from 45 Oak Avenue. This is your starting segment — the road runs directly south from your residence toward the downtown grid.

### Leg 2: 19th Street (2,156.5 m, ~21 min)
Turn right onto **19th Street** and head east. This is the longest segment of your walk at over 2 km. 19th Street runs through residential neighborhoods with sidewalks, tree canopies, and low traffic. You'll pass several blocks of housing and cross a few side streets.

### Leg 3: 20th St. (106.1 m, ~1 min)
Turn left at the intersection with **20th Street**. This is a short connector segment to align you with the Pearl Street corridor.

### Leg 4: 20th Street (565.6 m, ~7 min)
Continue on **20th Street** heading east. Pass through the neighborhood toward downtown. This section has sidewalk infrastructure and relatively low vehicle traffic.

### Leg 5: Pearl Street (216.2 m, ~3 min)
Turn onto **Pearl Street**. Pearl Street is a notable Boulder boulevard between downtown and the University Hill neighborhood. Sidewalks are well-maintained here.

### Leg 6: 18th Street (248.8 m, ~4 min)
Turn left on **18th Street** heading west. This segment takes you back toward the Broadway corridor.

### Leg 7: Canyon Boulevard (636.6 m, ~7 min)
Turn onto **Canyon Boulevard** and head west. Canyon Boulevard is a major east-west arterial that connects to Broadway. There are dedicated sidewalks along this stretch. As you approach Broadway, you'll see commercial developments and the entrance to your office district.

### Leg 8: Arrival at 1800 Broadway
Continue on Canyon Boulevard until you reach **1800 Broadway**. Your office building is visible from the intersection of Canyon Boulevard and Broadway.

## Route Characteristics

- **Sidewalk coverage**: Excellent throughout — all segments have dedicated pedestrian infrastructure
- **Traffic exposure**: Low — primarily residential streets with speed limits of 25–30 mph, but heavy pedestrian traffic in downtown sections
- **Shade**: Partial — some tree-lined sections on 19th Street; limited shade near Broadway commercial area
- **Accessibility**: Moderate — some crosswalks may require a slight uphill push on Canyon Boulevard
- **Safety**: Well-lit in downtown sections; slightly dimmer on 19th Street after dusk

## Best Time to Walk

- **Morning (7–9 AM)**: Cooler temperatures, less foot traffic on 19th Street
- **Evening (4–6 PM)**: Post-work energy, flat lighting for safety
- **Avoid**: Midday in summer (high UV at altitude) and during thunderstorm season (July–August)

## Weather Considerations

Boulder's high altitude (5,430 ft) means:
- **Sun intensity** is ~25% stronger than at sea level — sunscreen and sunglasses recommended
- **Temperature swings** can be 20°F+ between morning and afternoon
- **Afternoon thunderstorms** are common from July through August — plan indoor backup
- **Winter snow** can make sidewalks icy — bring traction aids for winter months
