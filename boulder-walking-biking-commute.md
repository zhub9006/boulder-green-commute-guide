### Walking Highlights
- Starts on quiet Oak Avenue (residential street)
- 19th Street is the main corridor — use the sidewalk
- Passes through downtown via Pearl Street Mall (car-free pedestrian zone)
- Scenic approach along Canyon Boulevard to Broadway

### Walking Tips
- Wear reflective gear for dawn/dusk on 19th Street
- Use crosswalk signals at intersections
- Grab coffee on Pearl Street to make the walk enjoyable
- Consider a walk+bus hybrid using RTD routes along Broadway

| 6 | 18th Street | 249 m | ~1 min |
| 7 | Canyon Boulevard | 637 m | ~1 min |

### Biking Highlights
- Protected bike lanes on 19th Street and Canyon Boulevard
- Dismount and walk your bike on Pearl Street Mall
- ~70% faster than walking
- Bike racks available near 1800 Broadway

### Biking Tips
- Use a U-lock at Broadway bike racks
- Check City of Boulder bike route maps seasonally
- Use lights year-round (especially Nov–Mar)
- Try BCycle day passes (~$9/day) if you do not own a bike
- Consider a folding bike for transit combinations on rainy days

## Boulder's Active Transportation Infrastructure

- Bike lanes and sharrows on major streets
- Pearl Street Mall — pedestrian-only downtown zone
- Canyon Boulevard — equipped cycling corridor
- RTD buses with bike racks for hybrid commutes
- BCycle Boulder — bike-share stations downtown ($9/day)
- Shiv Cycle Path — protected bike path, east Boulder to downtown
