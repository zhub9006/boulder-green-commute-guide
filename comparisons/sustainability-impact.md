### If All Boulder Residents Switched...

Boulder has approximately 100,000+ commuters. If even 10% switched from driving to walking or biking for a 4 km commute:
- **CO₂ reduction:** ~400,000 kg/year
- **Gasoline savings:** ~16,000 liters/year
- **Healthcare savings:** Significant reduction in cardiovascular disease, diabetes, obesity
- **Parking demand reduction:** Freeing up downtown space for businesses, green spaces, housing

## Boulder's Climate Goals

Boulder has committed to reducing community-wide greenhouse gas emissions to **net-zero by 2035** (Climate Action Plan). Sustainable commuting is a critical pillar:
- Transportation accounts for ~27% of Boulder's emissions
- Increasing active transportation mode share is a key strategy
- The City of Boulder invests heavily in bike infrastructure and pedestrian improvements

## Rebound Effect & Broader Benefits

Choosing to walk or bike doesn't just reduce your personal carbon footprint:
- 🏥 **Health:** Regular active commuting reduces risk of heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and depression
- 💰 **Economic:** Less spending on fuel, parking, and car maintenance
- 🏙️ **Urban:** More people walking/biking makes streets safer and more livable for everyone
- 🧠 **Mental:** Active commuting reduces stress and improves mood and productivity
