# Detailed Walking & Biking Commute Guide

> **Route:** 45 Oak Avenue ↔ 1800 Broadway, Boulder, CO  
> **Data source:** OpenStreetMap routing  
> **Last updated:** 2025

## 🚶 Walking Commute — Step-by-Step Guide

### Preparation
- **Footwear:** Comfortable walking shoes with good support
- **Weather:** Boulder weather changes quickly — carry a light rain layer
- **Timing:** Allow ~50 minutes to account for crosswalk waits and energy stops

### Detailed Directions

**1. Oak Avenue (97 m, ~1 min)**  
Head south on Oak Avenue from your home at 45 Oak Avenue. This residential street is quiet with low traffic — a calm start to your commute.

**2. 19th Street (2.16 km, ~21 min)**  
Turn onto 19th Street heading south. This is the longest segment of your walk. Use the sidewalk — this is a busy north-south arterial road. Be especially careful at intersections where turning vehicles may not yield to pedestrians.

> 💡 **Tip:** The sidewalk on the east side of 19th Street offers better sight lines with turning traffic.

**3. 20th St. (106 m, ~1 min)**  
Take a brief jog east on 20th Street. This quick connector connects you back to a southbound-ready position on 20th Street.

**4. 20th Street (566 m, ~7 min)**  
Continue south on 20th Street. The area transitions from residential to more downtown-oriented. Sidewalks are well-maintained.

**5. Pearl Street (216 m, ~3 min)**  
Enter the iconic **Pearl Street Mall** — Boulder's car-free pedestrian heart! This is a great spot to pause, grab coffee, or enjoy the street musicians. Browse the shops lining both sides of the mall.

> ☕ **Coffee stop suggestion:** Several cafes line Pearl Street between 15th and 17th Streets.

**6. 18th Street (249 m, ~4 min)**  
Walk east through a brief connector stretch. Cross at designated crosswalks — signals are well-timed here.

**7. Canyon Boulevard (637 m, ~6 min)**  
Enjoy the final stretch on **Canyon Boulevard**, a beautiful tree-lined boulevard that leads you right to Broadway and your office at 1800 Broadway. Watch for the bike lane crossing.

## 🚴 Biking Commute — Step-by-Step Guide

### Preparation
- **Bike:** Any bike works — standard, hybrid, or e-bike
- **Safety:** Wear a helmet, use front/rear lights (required by CO law at night)
- **Lock:** Bring a U-lock for securing your bike at Broadway
- **Timing:** Allow ~15 minutes — more if climbing into downtown during rush hour

### Detailed Directions

**1. Oak Avenue (97 m, ~1 min)**  
Exit your home heading south on Oak Avenue. This residential street is bike-friendly with low speed limits.

**2. 19th Street (2.16 km, ~3 min)**  
Turn onto 19th Street and use the **dedicated bike lane** heading south. This is your longest and most important cycling segment. The bike lane is separated from traffic, protected bike lane, but stay alert at intersections where right-turning vehicles may cross your path.

> ⚠️ **Caution:** 19th Street can be busy during 7:30–9:00 AM and 4:30–6:00 PM. The bike lane exists but drivers don't always check mirrors.

**3. 20th St. (106 m, ~1 min)**  
Brief jog east on 20th Street to reconnect with southbound flow.

**4. 20th Street (566 m, ~1 min)**  
Continue south in the bike lane. The road narrows slightly as you approach downtown — watch for shared-use signals.

**5. Pearl Street (216 m, ~1 min)**  
**Dismount and walk your bike.** The Pearl Street Mall is pedestrian-only; bikes are technically allowed but you must walk your bike near pedestrians. Use the parallel parking lane along the south edge of the mall to roll your bike through.

> 🔒 **Bike parking:** There are bike racks along Canyon Boulevard near 18th Street if you need to lock up temporarily.

**6. 18th Street (249 m, ~1 min)**  
Brief connector — rejoin your bike if you ditched it, or continue walking it through the mall zone.

**7. Canyon Boulevard (637 m, ~1 min)**  
Enjoy the **protected bike lane** on Canyon Boulevard as it sweeps you into downtown Boulder and right up to Broadway. This is the most scenic part of the ride — look up at the Flatirons!

## 🔄 Combining Walking & Biking (Hybrid Approach)

Many Boulder commuters use a **hybrid strategy**:

1. **Walk** from Oak Avenue to a bike rack near 19th & Canyon (~10 min)
2. **BCycle** (Boulder's bike-share) south on Canyon Boulevard to downtown (~5 min)
3. **Walk** the final blocks through the Pearl Street Mall to your office (~5 min)

**Total time:** ~20–25 minutes  
**Cost:** $9/day for BCycle, or free if you own a bike  
**Best for:** Days when you don't want to carry a bike through the pedestrian mall

## 🌤️ Seasonal Considerations

| Season | Walking | Biking |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Spring (Mar–May)** | Ideal — mild temps, occasional rain | Great — watch for slippery roads after rain |
| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | Hot midday — go early or late | Excellent — long days, but stay hydrated |
| **Fall (Sep–Nov)** | Perfect — comfortable temps | Great — shorter days, use lights |
| **Winter (Dec–Feb)** | Cold & snow — use traction shoes | Challenging — ice on Canyon Blvd. Use studded tires or switch to transit |
