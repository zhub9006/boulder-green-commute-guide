# Sustainability Impact: 45 Oak Avenue ↔ 1800 Broadway

*Calculated for Boulder, CO residents considering car-free commuting*

---

## Environmental Impact

### Per Trip Savings (vs. Driving)
- **CO₂ saved:** ~1.5 kg per one-way trip
- **Fuel saved:** ~0.5 liters per trip

### Annual Savings (250 workdays, round trip daily)
- **CO₂ saved:** ~375 kg/year (~827 lbs)
- **Fuel saved:** ~125 liters/year (~33 gallons)
- **Equivalent:** Taking a car off the road for ~1 month per year

### Broader Context
- A single car produces ~4.6 metric tons of CO₂/year
- This commute switch eliminates ~8% of your transportation carbon footprint
- Boulder's grid is ~60% renewable, amplifying the benefit

- **Calories burned per round trip:** ~480 kcal
- **Annual calories burned:** ~120,000 kcal (~34 lbs of fat equivalent)
- **Health benefits:** Reduced cardiovascular risk, improved mood, better sleep, lower stress

### Biking Commute
- **Calories burned per one-way:** ~300 kcal
- **Calories burned per round trip:** ~600 kcal

## Boulder-Specific Advantages

1. **Flat terrain** — Boulder's core is unusually flat, making bike commuting easier than most cities
2. **Bike infrastructure** — Protected bike lanes on major corridors
3. **Bike-share** — BCycle stations throughout downtown
4. **Public transit integration** — RTD buses accommodate bikes
5. **Weather** — 300+ sunny days/year; brief but intense snow in winter
6. **Culture** — Boulder is one of America's most bike-friendly cities; you'll find many fellow green commuters
