# 💡 Sustainable Travel Tips for Boulder Commuters

## General Tips

### 🚶 Walking
- **Layer up**: Boulder's mornings can be 15–20°F cooler than downtown
- **Walk on the sidewalk**: Always use sidewalks; cross at marked crosswalks
- **Carry a lightweight rain shell**: Boulder weather changes quickly
- **Enjoy Pearl Street**: It's a pedestrian mall — the perfect place to walk and browse

### 🚴 Biking
- **Use the bike lanes**: Boulder has over 200 miles of paved bike paths
- **Walk your bike on Pearl Street Mall**: Bikes are not allowed in the pedestrian mall section
- **Lock up at Canyon Blvd Stations**: B-Cycle docking stations are available near Broadway
- **Winter riding**: Fenders and lights are essential; roads can be icy
- **E-bikes**: Consider an e-bike if you deal with Colorado's 7,500 ft elevation — it makes hills trivial

### 🚌 Transit Alternatives
- **RTD Bus Routes**: Several routes connect 80304 to downtown Boulder
- **B-Cycle**: Bike-share stations on both ends of this commute
- **Carpool**: If you drive, consider forming a carpool to reduce emissions

### 🌿 Carbon Reduction
- Walking or biking **2 days/week** saves ~40 kg CO₂/year
- Walking or biking **5 days/week** saves ~100 kg CO₂/year
- Combining cycling with RTD bus = zero-emission commute!

### 📅 Seasonal Recommendations
| Season | Best Mode | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Spring (Mar–May) | Either | Beautiful weather; trails are dry |
| Summer (Jun–Aug) | Biking | Warm, low wind; bring water |
| Fall (Sep–Nov) | Either | Cool temps; golden aspen leaves |
| Winter (Dec–Feb) | E-bike | Ice and snow; bike lanes may be narrow |
