# 💡 Sustainable Travel Tips for Boulder Commuters

> **For:** Boulder residents considering walking or biking for their daily commute  
> **Date:** July 2026  | **Source:** City of Boulder Transportation, RTD, OSM  

---

## 🌸 Spring & Summer (April–September)

### Walking Tips
- **Hydrate early** — Even on mild mornings, bring a water bottle. Boulder's altitude (5,430 ft) means UV exposure is 25% higher than sea level.
- **Sun protection** — Wear SPF 30+ sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat. The high-altitude sun is deceptively strong.
- **Afternoon thunderstorms** — June–August afternoons often bring sudden storms. Check the forecast and carry a lightweight rain jacket.
- **Enjoy the Mall** — The Pearl Street Mall is a pedestrian-only zone — the safest and most enjoyable part of the walk. Stop for a coffee or gelato!
- **Best walking times:** 6–9 AM (cool, less crowded), after 5 PM (avoid peak sun)

### Biking Tips
- **Use the bike lanes** — 19th and 20th Streets have dedicated bike lanes that run along the east side.
- **Helmet is essential** — Boulder is a bike-friendly city, but the Canyon Boulevard corridor moves fast.
- **Lights even in daylight** — Boulder's bike-share (BCycle) stations have lights; always use them.
- **Avoid Pearl St Mall on bikes** — Dismount and walk your bike through this zone. It's safer for you and pedestrians.
- **Lock your bike properly** — 1800 Broadway has bike racks, but use a U-lock for maximum security.

### Seasonal Perks
- 🌻 Boulder Creek Path — Parallel to your route; scenic alternative on weekends
- 🏔️ Flatiron views — Some segments offer iconic views of the Flatirons
- 🌡️ Locals bike year-round — You'll see fellow commuters in all weather!

## 🍂 Fall & Winter (October–March)

### Walking Tips
- **Layer up** — Morning temps can be 20°F in winter. Start with a base layer, add insulation, and top with a windproof shell.
- **Microspikes for ice** — The 19th Street sidewalk can ice over. Wear Yaks or similar traction devices.
- **Reflective gear** — Daylight is limited. Wear a reflective vest or use a blinking rear light.
- **Indoor warm-up** — Use the **Ku Cha House of Tea** on Pearl Street as a warming stop on cold mornings.
- **Best walking times:** 10 AM–3 PM (warmest hours, best sunlight)

### Biking Tips
- **Fat-tire or e-bike recommended** — Regular bikes struggle on snow/ice. If you bike in winter, consider an e-bike or studded winter tires.
- **Salt claims on 19th Street** — The city clears the bike lane on 19th St, but 20th Street may have residual ice.
- **Stay visible** — Front and rear lights required by Boulder law, even during the day. Most bike thefts happen in winter when bikes are left outside.
- **Pearl St Mall** — Still walk your bike. But now watch for wet, slippery crowds.
- **Alternative in bad weather** — Switch to the **BCycle** electric bike share system. The Municipal Building e-bike station is closest to your office.

## 📅 Year-Round Best Practices

### The "2-Day Challenge" — Start Here!
1. **Week 1:** Walk or bike just 2 days per week (minimal commitment)
2. **Week 2:** Increase to 3 days
3. **Week 3:** Try 4 days — you'll notice better energy and sleep
4. **Week 4+:** Find your rhythm — most people settle into 3–5 days

### Pre-Commute Checklist
- [ ] Check the weather (Boulder weather can change rapidly!)
- [ ] Backpack ready: water bottle, rain layer, snacks
- [ ] Bike check: tires, brakes, chain (if biking)
- [ ] Phone charged & packed (navigation, emergency)
- [ ] Walking shoes laid out or helmet on handlebar

### Mixing Modes (Multimodal)
- **Bike + RTD Bus:** Bike to a nearby RTD stop (Arapahoe & 15th), load bike on bus rack, ride to Broadway
- **Walk + BCycle:** Walk the 19th St portion, pick up a BCycle e-bike from the Municipal Building station, ride the last 0.5 mi
- **Bike + Walk:** Bike the fastest portions (19th St, 20th St), walk the Pearl St Mall section

## 🛤️ Alternative Routes & Connectors

### The Boulder Creek Path (Scenic Alternative)
A beautiful, mostly flat path that runs alongside Boulder Creek. It's ~0.5 mi longer but far more scenic than the urban route. Perfect for weekend commuting or hot summer days.

**Leo Outfitting Loop (since you're into outdoors):**
- Exit 19th Street east to the creek path
- Follow the creek path south
- Reconnect to Canyon Blvd near Broadway
- **Total extra distance:** ~0.5 mi / ~5 min

### The 13th Street Connector (Faster Alternative)
If you're in a hurry and it's a weekday morning:
- Turn east on 19th St to 13th St
- 13th St has a protected bike lane heading south
- Connects to Broadway faster than the Pearl St Mall zone
- **Time savings:** ~2–3 min, but less scenic
