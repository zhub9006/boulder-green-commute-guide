# 💡 Sustainable Travel Tips for Boulder

## Year-Round Commuting Advice

### 🌱 Be a Smart Commuter

1. **Layer up** — Boulder's 5,431 ft elevation means temperature swings of 20°F+ between morning and afternoon
2. **Check the wind** — Canyon Blvd can be breezy; dress for wind chill in winter/spring
3. **Use a pannier** — More comfortable than a shoulder bag on a bike; keeps hands free
4. **Shower at work** — The downtown office area likely has shower facilities
5. **Ice you bike** — No, seriously, ice your tires for better grip on winter mornings
6. **Clock-check** — Morning rush is 7:30–8:30 AM on 19th St; flexible start times help

### 🌞 Summer Tips (Jun–Aug)
- Bring a water bottle — afternoon temps reach 90°F+
- Wear sunscreen and a hat
- Use a headlight for evening commutes
- Dust on Canyon Blvd can be slippery when dry

### ❄️ Winter Tips (Nov–Mar)
- Studded tires are a game-changer for ice
- Allow +10–15 min extra for snow/ice on 19th St
- Wear reflective gear — daylight ends by 5 PM
- Watch for "black ice" on shaded stretches of 19th St
- If it's too dangerous, the RTD bus route 200 is your backup

### 🌸 Spring & Fall Tips
- Peak biking season — enjoy!
- Watch for spring rain making sidewalks slick
- Fall leaves on Canyon Blvd are beautiful but can be slippery
- Layer up for cool mornings; peel off by midday

### 🚴 Bike Maintenance Checklist
- [ ] Check tire pressure weekly (altitude affects PSI)
- [ ] Test brakes monthly
- [ ] Lubricate chain every 2 weeks
- [ ] Check lights/bell before evening rides
- [ ] Inspect helmet fit annually
