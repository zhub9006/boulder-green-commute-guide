# 💡 Sustainable Commute Tips for Boulder

Your guide to making green commuting work year-round in Boulder, Colorado.

---

## 🌦️ Seasonal Commuting Tips

### Spring (April – May)
- **Pro:** Mud season is ending, temps are warming (50–70°F)
- **Con:** Occasional rain — keep a lightweight rain jacket handy
- **Tip:** Start biking! Trail systems are opening up, and the UV index is moderate
- **Walking tip:** Cub Creek Trail is beautiful in spring

### Summer (June – August)
- **Pro:** Long daylight hours, warm weather (75–95°F)
- **Con:** Intense afternoon heat and UV (index 8–10+)
- **Tip:** Bike to work early (before 9 AM) or late (after 5 PM) to avoid heat
- **Must-have:** Sunscreen, hat, hydration pack or water bottle
- **Tip:** Foothills Trail offers a scenic, shaded walking/cycling alternative

### Fall (September – October)
- **Pro:** Best biking and walking weather (50–75°F), golden aspens
- **Con:** Shorter days — carry a headlamp if coming home after dark
- **Tip:** This is peak commuting season — take advantage!
- **Walking tip:** Pearl Street Mall is lively in fall, great for walking meetings

### Winter (November – March)
- **Pro:** No bugs, crisp air, snow on the peaks
- **Con:** Cold (20–45°F), snow/ice on roads, short days
- **Bike tip:** Fenders are essential; consider studded tires for ice
- **Walking tip:** Downtown Boulder stays clear of snow; residential streets may lag
- **Alternative:** RTD Bus Route 201/202 serves this corridor — great winter backup
- **Tip:** Layer up! Base layer + insulating layer + wind/waterproof shell

## 🔒 Safety Tips

### General
- Always use lights (front white, rear red) at night or in low visibility
- Wear a helmet when biking — Colorado law for riders under 18; recommended for all
- Be predictable: signal turns, use bike lanes, make eye contact with drivers
- Keep valuables secure: use locks on bikes, keep bags close when walking
- Know the route: familiarize yourself with safe crosswalks and bike lanes

### Walking-Specific
- Use crosswalks and obey pedestrian signals
- On 19th Street, be especially alert at intersections
- Stay on sidewalks — avoid walking in the street
- In winter, watch for black ice on sidewalks, especially near Pearl Street

### Biking-Specific
- Use the Canyon Boulevard bike lane whenever possible — it's the safest segment
- On 19th Street, take the lane when necessary and be visible
- Watch for pedestrians at cross streets (Pearl, 18th, Broadway)
- Lock bike with a U-lock + cable at 1800 Broadway bike racks

## 🎒 Gear Recommendations

### For Walkers
| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Waterproof walking shoes | Keep feet dry in rain/snow |
| Lightweight daypack (15–20L) | Carry laptop, lunch, layers |
| Reflective vest or clip-on light | Visibility on darker days |
| Reusable water bottle | Stay hydrated year-round |
| Sunglasses & sunscreen | UV protection even in winter |

### For Bikers
| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Bicycle helmet | Head protection (CO law compliance + safety) |
| Front & rear lights | Visibility day and night |
| Fenders (full-coverage) | Keep mud and spray off you and your clothes |
| Commuter pannier or backpack | Carry work items hands-free |
| Lock (U-lock + cable) | Secure bike at downtown racks |
| Gloves (full-finger) | Grip and warmth in cold weather |

## 🚲 Boulder Bike Resources

- **B-Cycle Boulder:** 35+ stations across the city; $12/month for unlimited 30-min rides. Use for one-way trips or supplement your route.
- **City of Boulder Bike Map:** [bouldercolorado.gov/biketrails](https://www.bouldercolorado.gov/biketrails) — Download the PDF for all-ages bike route maps.
- **Community Cyclist:** 2450 63rd St — Local bike shop for maintenance and tune-ups.
- **Pedal the Peak:** Facebook group for group rides and commuter support.

## 🌱 Quick Wins for the Environment

Even if you can't commit to a full green commute, every bit helps:

1. **Walk or bike 1 day/week:** Saves ~400 kg CO₂/year
2. **Walk or bike 3 days/week:** Saves ~1,200 kg CO₂/year
3. **Combine with transit:** Bike to RTD bus, ride, walk last mile = flexibility + sustainability
4. **Carpool on bad-weather days:** Even splitting a car cuts emissions in half vs. solo driving
5. **Advocate:** Tell Boulder City Council you support bike lanes and pedestrian infrastructure!
