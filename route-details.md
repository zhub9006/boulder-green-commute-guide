## Street-Level Safety Notes

### Oak Avenue
- Residential, low-speed street
- No bike lane — use caution near driveway entries
- Good lighting in evening hours

### 19th Street
- ** busiest segment of the route **
- Wide sidewalk on both sides
- Bike lane present but narrows near intersections
- Highest risk: right-hook turns at intersections
- Better visibility in morning (east-facing for walkers heading south)

### 20th Street
- Connector streets — lower volumes
- Good sidewalk infrastructure
- Short segments make navigation easy

### Pearl Street Mall
- **Car-free zone** — safest part of the route
- High pedestrian volume, especially weekends
- Bikes allowed but must yield to pedestrians
- Multiple cafes and rest stops
- Street performers may occupy sidewalk space

### 18th Street
- Short connector — minimal risk
- Well-timed pedestrian signals
- Brief overlap with bike lane zone

### Canyon Boulevard
- Tree-lined boulevard with wide sidewalks
- Protected bike lane on the east side
- Scenic approach to downtown
- Watch for pedestrians entering/exiting shops
