## 🚲 Commute-Friendly Features of This Neighborhood

### Why Oak Avenue is Great for Green Commuting

1. **Low-traffic residential street** — Oak Avenue itself is quiet and safe for pedestrians and cyclists
2. **Direct southbound route** — The 19th Street corridor provides a straight path from north Boulder to downtown
3. **Proximity to Canyon Boulevard** — One of Boulder's premier multi-use corridors (just 636 m from the office)
4. **Boulder's bike culture** — The city supports cycling with dedicated lanes, bike boxes, and bike-friendly infrastructure
5. **Walkable destination area** — Broadway and downtown Boulder offer shops, cafes, and services within walking distance of the office

## 📋 Broader Boulder Context

Boulder consistently ranks as one of the most bike-friendly and walkable cities in the United States. Key facts:

- **Boulder's Walk Score:** ~55 (Somewhat Walkable) — improving with ongoing downtown investments
- **Bike network:** Over 100 miles of dedicated bike lanes and multi-use paths
- **Climate:** 300+ days of sunshine per year — ideal for walking and biking year-round
- **Elevation:** ~5,430 ft (1,655 m) — dry air, thin air at altitude; hydrate on longer rides
- **B-Cycle:** Station-based bike share system with multiple downtown stations
