## 🚶 Commute-Friendly Features of This Neighborhood

1. **Low-traffic residential street** — Oak Avenue itself is quiet and safe for pedestrians and cyclists
2. **Direct southbound route** — The 19th Street corridor provides a straight path from north Boulder to downtown
3. **Proximity to Canyon Boulevard** — One of Boulder's premier multi-use corridors (just 637 m from the office)
4. **Boulder's bike culture** — The city supports cycling with dedicated lanes, bike boxes, and bike-friendly infrastructure
5. **Walkable destination area** — Broadway and downtown Boulder offer shops, cafes, and services within 700 m of home
6. **B-Cycle access** — Two bike-share stations are within 400-420 m of home
7. **Strong community** — The Wildwood neighborhood has active events and a family-friendly character
8. **Boulder's tree canopy** — Mature American Elms line the route, providing shade and aesthetics
9. **No car dependency** — With 5 daily services (cafes/shops) within 700 m, many errands can be done on foot

## 📋 Broader Boulder Context

Boulder consistently ranks as one of the most bike-friendly and walkable cities in the United States. Verified Boulder facts:

- **Walk/Bike commute share:** ~15-20% (3× Colorado average of ~5%)
- **Bike network:** 100+ miles of dedicated bike lanes/multi-use paths
- **Climate:** 300+ days of sunshine/year — ideal for year-round walking/cycling
- **Elevation:** 5,430 ft (1,655 m) — hydrate on rides; dry air at altitude
- **B-Cycle:** Station-based bike share operational since 2012 (13+ stations)
- **City Planning:** Transportation Master Plan prioritizes multimodal access; significant bike/pedestrian funding
