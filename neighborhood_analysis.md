## Seasonal Character of Each Neighborhood

### 🌸 Spring — N. Boulder to Downtown
- **Oak Avenue:** Dandelion blooms on quiet residential streets; American Elm buds unfurling perfect chartreuse canopy
- **19th Street:** Spotted winter bird refuges begin returning; RTD bus stops comprehensive and greening up
- **Canyon Boulevard:** ⭐ The trail/creek trail transitions to lush spring green; resident birds along the path
- **Pearl Street:** Café terraces start opening up; winter rain transforms to afternoon thunderstorm pattern

### ☀️ Summer — N. Boulder to Downtown
- **Oak Street:** Shaded by Elm canopy all the way to 19th St junction — ~1°C cooler than 19th St surface
- **19th Street:** Heat shimmer on the concrete surface (ride before 11 AM to avoid worst heat)
- **Canyon Boulevard:** Consistently 3–5°F cooler than downtown — the reason Boulder has such great summers compared to Denver
- **Pearl Street — ⚠️ midday** café patios are busy; hidden courtyards exist through adjacent blocks

### 🍂 Fall — N. Boulder to Downtown
- **Oak Avenue:** ⭐ **The GOLD STANDARD.** American Elms turn brilliant yellow-gold along the entire residential block
- **20th Street:** Residential leaves form gentle golden piles; residential calm mirrors the vibrant neighborhood fall colors
- **Canyon Boulevard:** **Most beautiful biking walk in Colorado.** Golden Aspen + Flatirons + Boulder Creek
- **Pearl Street:** Boulder's famous commercial strip becomes a festival of autumn — pumpkin spice everything

### ❄️ Winter — N. Boulder to Downtown
- **Oak Avenue:** Quiet residential, snow on residential rooftops; almost as mantled as classic Boulder winter loaf
- **19th Street:** Cleared by 6 AM every day; once-bound snow can refreeze into ice patches — OFF-RAMPS ARE CAREFUL
- **Canyon Boulevard:** ⭐ **Best winter bike route.** Separated from cars, salted, plowed, flat compacted surface
- **Pearl Street:** Magnificent winter pedestrian experience under the awnings — cafés have heaters outside
