## 🌍 Why This Matters for Boulder

Boulder is a leader in sustainable transportation, and this commute is a perfect example of why:

- 🏙 **Walkable city design**: Boulder's grid street system and grid spacing (16th–32nd St.) make vertical commutes across the city very efficient
- 🌱 **Green commitment**: Boulder aims for carbon neutrality by 2030 — choosing to walk or bike for this commute saves approximately **200 kg of CO₂ per year** (equivalent to ~50 gallons of gasoline)
- 🏔 **Beautiful scenery**: The route passes through some of Boulder's most charming neighborhoods, from the tree-lined 19th Street to the vibrant Pearl Street Mall area
- 🌡 **Four-season city**: Boulder has 300+ days of sunshine per year — walk/bike year-round with minimal weather disruption

## 💡 Sustainable Commuting Tips for Boulder Residents

1. **Layer up**: Boulder weather changes fast — bring layers for walks in spring/fall
2. **Use the PATH**: The Boulder PATH trail system connects many neighborhoods — consider mixing your route with trail segments
3. **Window shopping**: Pearl Street Mall is right on this route — great for a mid-morning stroll
4. **Bike share**: Boulder offers B-cycle bike-share stations near 18th & Canyon for one-way trips
5. **Commute with neighbors**: Walking groups and bike pools are popular in Boulder-area neighborhoods
6. **Track your impact**: Use apps like Strava, Komoot, or Boulder County's carbon footprint tracker to measure your green commute
