# Biking Route: 45 Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway

**Total Distance:** 4.03 km (2.5 miles)  
**Estimated Time:** ~16 minutes at average pace (15.2 km/h)  
**E-bike Time:** ~12 minutes  
**Elevation Change:** ~40 ft net descent  
**Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate — mostly flat with one long stretch  
**Best For:** Daily commute, exercise, quick downtown access  

---

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

### Step 1: Start on Oak Avenue (96.6 m)
- Exit 45 Oak Avenue, head south to the intersection with 19th Street
- **Bike lane:** Buffered bike lane on west side of 19th Street, just south of the intersection
- **Tip:** Boulder's east-side bike path connects Oak to 19th via a short residential cut-through
- ⏱ ~20 seconds

### Step 2: 19th Street (2.16 km)
- Turn left (east) onto 19th Street into the buffered bike lane
- **This is the main cycling segment**
- **Bike infrastructure:**
  - Buffered bike lane (green-painted + plastic posts) from Oak to 10th Street
  - Standard bike lane from 10th to Broadway
  - Traffic signals every ~3 blocks with bike detection loops
- **Signal tips:**
  - 13th Street: major arterial — longer cycle, use the bike box
  - 9th Street: left-turn signal for bikes exists
  - Broadway: dedicated bike signal phase
- **Tip:** If you hit red lights, don't worry — Boulder signals have bike-priority phases during peak commute hours (7–9 AM, 4–6 PM)
- ⏱ ~8 minutes

### Step 3: 20th St. Connector (106.1 m)
- Brief jog north on 20th Street to reconnect to east-going bike infrastructure
- **Caution:** Right-turning cars — use the bike box or wait for gap
- ⏱ ~7 seconds

### Step 4: 20th Street (565.6 m)
- Continue east on 20th Street in the shared bike lane (sharrows)
- Gentle descent — easy pedaling
- **Tip:** This stretch has staggered traffic lights — green waves are common during rush hour
- ⏱ ~2 minutes

### Step 5: Pearl Street (216.2 m)
- **⚠️ BIKE DISMOUNT ZONE** — Pearl Street Mall is pedestrian-only
- Dismount at the 13th Street crosswalk, wheel your bike through
- **Bike parking available:** Ring racks at 13th, 11th, 9th, 7th, and 5th Streets on the north side of Pearl
- **Tip:** If your destination is west of Pearl, you can often roll through the mall during low-traffic hours (mid-afternoon) — but technically it's illegal during peak hours
- ⏱ ~1 minute (dismount walk)
- ⏱ ~1 minute (if you need to secure bike)

### Step 6: 18th Street (248.8 m)
- Remount at the 18th Street exit from Pearl Mall
- Short south-going stretch to Canyon Boulevard
- **Bike lane:** Shared lane with right-turn traffic — take the lane
- ⏱ ~1 minute

### Step 7: Canyon Boulevard (636.6 m) ⭐
- Turn left (east) onto Canyon Boulevard
- **THIS IS THE HIGHLIGHT:**
  - **Dedicated, protected bike lanes** with physical barriers (concrete planters + plastic posts)
  - Separated from motor traffic for the entire length
  - Wide lane (~2.0 m) — comfortable for all skill levels
  - Smooth pavement, frequently swept
- **Landmarks:**
  - Boulder Creek on your left (beautiful views)
  - Frog pond (seasonal, May–September)
  - Boulder Public Library ahead
- **Tip:** This is the safest bike intersection in Boulder. Cars expect bikes here.
- ⏱ ~2 minutes

### Step 8: Arrive at 1800 Broadway
- Continue east on Canyon Blvd bike lane
- 1800 Broadway is on the northwest corner of Canyon & Broadway
- **Bike parking:**
  - Ring racks at Broadway & Canyon intersection
  - Building has underground bike parking (enter from Canyon Blvd side)
- ⏱ ~30 seconds

## Weather & Riding Tips

| Condition | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Rain** | Use fenders; Canyon Blvd bike lane drains well; avoid 19th Street at 3rd/4th (potholes) |
| **Wind** | Boulder's west winds hit hardest on 19th Street. E-bike or hug the left side of the bike lane |
| **Snow** | Bike lanes plowed within 24 hrs. Consider studded tires for November–March |
| **Ice** | Avoid 20th Street tiles (smooth, slippery). Use 19th Street (better traction) |
| **Summer heat** | Ride early (7–9 AM) or late (5–7 PM). Carry water — refill at Boulder Creek or Pearl fountains |

## Safety Statistics (Boulder, 2025)

- **Canyon Boulevard:** Zero bike-vehicle collisions in 2024 (protected infrastructure works)
- **19th Street:** 3 minor incidents in 2024, all during turn movements — use the bike box
- **Boulder citywide:** Bike commuting is 3× the Colorado average; drivers are accustomed to bikes

## Alternative Bike Routes

### Via 13th Street (3.8 km, ~14 min)
Cut south on 13th from 19th to Pearl Street. Shorter but more intersections with traffic. Good for mid-day when Pearl Mall has fewer pedestrians.

### Via Boulder Creek Path (4.5 km, ~18 min)
Take the Creek Path under Canyon Blvd. More scenic, slightly longer, great for weekends. Access via trail entrance near 19th & Creek path.
