# 🚴‍♀️ Biking Route: Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway

## Overview
- **Distance:** 4.0 km (2.5 miles)
- **Time:** ~16 minutes
- **Calories Burned:** ~110 calories
- **Difficulty:** Easy — Flat terrain, bike-friendly infrastructure

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Head south on Oak Avenue** (97 m, ~1 min)
   - Start at 45 Oak Avenue
   - Safe, quiet residential street
   - Accelerate onto the main route

2. **Turn left onto 19th Street** (2.2 km, ~9 min)
   - Main biking corridor
   - Bike lane available
   - Moderate traffic — stay alert
   - Flow with traffic, follow road markings

3. **Turn right onto 20th St.** (106 m, ~1 min)
   - Quick connector
   - Short distance

4. **Continue east on 20th Street** (566 m, ~2 min)
   - Busier downtown street
   - Watch for car doors opening
   - Use bike lane if available

5. **Turn right onto Pearl Street** (216 m, ~1 min)
   - Historic area — slow down for pedestrians
   - Brick surface may be bumpy
   - Share space with walkers and tourists
   - Dismount if crowded

6. **Turn left onto 18th Street** (249 m, ~1 min)
   - Head toward Canyon Boulevard
   - Moderate traffic
   - Good visibility

7. **Turn right onto Canyon Boulevard** (637 m, ~3 min)
   - 🌟 Best segment! Dedicated bike/pedestrian path along Boulder Creek
   - Beautiful scenery
   - Flat and fast
   - Watch for wildlife and creek crossings

8. **Arrive at 1800 Broadway** — your destination

## Biking Infrastructure Notes
- **19th Street:** Buffered bike lane on the south side
- **20th Street:** Shared lane markings (sharrows)
- **Pearl Street:** Pedestrian priority — dismount or slow to walking speed
- **18th Street:** Low-traffic connector with bike awareness
- **Canyon Boulevard:** Wide multi-use path — the crown jewel of Boulder's bike network

## Safety Tips
- Always wear a helmet — Boulder's fine for not wearing one ($25+)
- Use hand signals for turns
- Front and rear lights required at night
- Average speed: 12-15 mph on this route
- Dry-season: Watch for sand and gravel on roads
- Monsoon season: Drainage grates can be slippery
- Carry a mini-pump and spare tube
- RTD buses have front bike racks if you need to mix modes

## Gear Recommendations
- **Minimum:** Any bike with a bell, helmet, and lights
- **Recommended:** Well-maintained hybrid or city bike with fenders
- **Nice to have:** Pannier bag for carrying work items, laptop sleeve
- **Optional:** Front basket for groceries on return trips

## Weather Considerations
- **Boulder averages:** 300+ sunny days per year
- **Summer:** Hot and dry — bring water, wear sunscreen
- **Fall:** Perfect cycling temperatures
- **Winter:** Cold and snowy — studded tires and layers
- **Spring:** Unpredictable — carry a rain jacket
- **Year-round:** Fenders recommended for wet conditions
