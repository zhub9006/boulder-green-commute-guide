### Segment 3: 20th St. (106 m)

### Segment 4: 20th Street (566 m)

### Segment 5: Pearl Street (216 m)
- **Direction:** Turn onto Pearl Street
- **Surface:** Downtown; bike lane / shared use
- **Traffic:** Heavy pedestrian and vehicle traffic
- **Tip:** **Dismount and walk your bike** through the Pearl Street Mall pedestrian zone if preferred. Bike racks are available along Pearl. Or use the designated bike lane if confident.

### Segment 6: 18th Street (249 m)
- **Direction:** Continue through the downtown core
- **Surface:** Road with bike infrastructure
- **Traffic:** Moderate — signalized intersections
- **Tip:** Use the bike lane where available; signal yourself at intersections

### Segment 7: Canyon Boulevard (637 m)
- **Direction:** Turn onto Canyon Boulevard, head toward Broadway
- **Surface:** Road with wide shoulder
- **Traffic:** Moderate to Heavy — one of Boulder's busiest roads
- **Tip:** Canyon Blvd has a wide shoulder — use it. Be visible with lights and bright clothing. The intersection with Broadway is the trickiest part.

### Arrival: 1800 Broadway
- Follow Canyon Boulevard to Broadway
- Your office is at 1800 Broadway
- Bike parking is available downtown

## Seasonal Biking Tips

### Spring & Fall
- ✅ Ideal conditions — dress in layers
- 🌧️ Keep rain gear handy

### Summer
- ☀️ Start early to avoid afternoon heat
- 💧 Carry water — dehydration risk at 5,430 ft elevation
- 🧴 Sunscreen recommended

### Winter
- ❄️ Snow and ice can make roads treacherous
- 🔦 Use front and rear lights — shorter daylight hours
- ⛑️ Consider studded tires for ice
- Some Boulder cyclists use studded tires year-round
