# Biking Route: Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway

## Summary
- **Distance:** 4.0 km (2.5 miles)
- **Duration:** ~7 minutes
- **Elevation gain:** ~50m (164 ft)
- **Difficulty:** Easy (minimal elevation, bike-friendly streets)

## Detailed Turn-by-Turn Directions

### Segment 1: Oak Avenue (97 m, ~16 seconds)
- Start at 45 Oak Avenue, heading south
- Oak Avenue is a quiet residential street — very bike-friendly
- Short segment to 19th Street

### Segment 2: 19th Street (2.16 km, ~3.5 minutes)
- Turn right (southbound) onto 19th Street
- Main arterial with bike lanes or sharrows
- Moderate traffic during rush hours — stay alert
- Well-maintained road surface

### Segment 3: 20th Street (566 m, ~1.2 minutes)
- Turn left onto 20th Street
- Less traffic than 19th Street
- Good bike infrastructure

### Segment 4: Pearl Street (216 m, ~26 seconds)
- Turn right onto Pearl Street
- Busy downtown pedestrian area — slow down and yield to pedestrians
-鼓动 Use caution near the Pearl Street Mall
- Short segment only

### Segment 5: 18th Street (249 m, ~37 seconds)
- Turn left onto 18th Street
- Quiet connector street
- Good visibility

### Segment 6: Canyon Boulevard (637 m, ~58 seconds)
- Continue onto Canyon Boulevard
- **Most scenic segment** — paved multi-use path along Boulder Creek
- Popular with both cyclists and pedestrians
- Wide path allows easy passing
- Watch for pedestrians and joggers

### Arrival: 1800 Broadway
- End at 1800 Broadway, office area near CU Boulder
- Bike parking available in the area

## Bike Infrastructure Along the Route

- **19th Street:** Sharrows (shared lane markings) present
- **Canyon Boulevard:** Dedicated multi-use path (paved, separated from car traffic)
- **20th Street:** Bike-friendly with lower traffic volumes
- **Bike parking:** Available near 1800 Broadway

## Tips for Cyclists
- **Bike maintenance:** Check tires and brakes before each ride; Boulder's bike paths can be sandy near the creek
- **Helmet:** Always recommended (and required for under-18 in Colorado)
- **Lighting:** If riding in low light, use front and rear lights — Canyon Boulevard path can be dim
- **Dashcam/visor cam:** Useful for documenting any incidents on 19th Street
- **Passing pedestrians:** On the Canyon Boulevard path, pass on the left and announce "passing on your left"
- **Locking up:** Use U-locks on 18th or 19th Street; avoid locking to Canyon Boulevard driftwood or signs

## Seasonal Notes
- **Spring:** Bike paths along the creek may have standing water or mud — wider tires helpful
- **Summer:** Hot pavement can be tough on hands and seat; carry water
- **Fall:** Leaf debris on paths can be slippery — slow down on turns
- **Winter:** Canyon Boulevard path may accumulate ice; consider Road ID or ice cleats for pedals. 19th Street should be plowed.
