# 🚲 Biking Route: 45 Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway

## Route Summary

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Total Distance** | 4,026 m (2.5 miles) |
| **Estimated Time** | ~16 minutes at casual pace (15 km/h) |
| **Terrain** | Flat Boulder terrain; slight elevation near Canyon Boulevard |
| **Bike Infrastructure** | Good — bike lanes on 19th St., Creek Path available |

---

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

### Segment 1: Oak Avenue
- **Distance:** 97 m
- **Time:** ~15 sec
- **Direction:** Head south on Oak Avenue
- **Notes:** Short residential start. Watch for driveways.

### Segment 2: 19th Street (Southbound)
- **Distance:** 2,157 m
- **Time:** ~1 min 35 sec
- **Direction:** Continue south on 19th Street
- **Notes:** Bike-friendly corridor. Use the bike lane. Moderate traffic — stay visible.

### Segment 3: 20th Street (Connector)
- **Distance:** 106 m
- **Time:** ~12 sec
- **Direction:** Brief connector
- **Notes:** Quick yield to pedestrian traffic.

### Segment 4: 20th Street (Southbound)
- **Distance:** 566 m
- **Time:** ~55 sec
- **Direction:** Continue on 20th Street
- **Notes:** Calmer than 19th. Good for beginners to Boulder biking.

### Segment 5: Pearl Street (Westbound)
- **Distance:** 216 m
- **Time:** ~25 sec
- **Direction:** Turn west onto Pearl Street
- **Notes:** Pearl Street is a pedestrian mall — dismount and walk your bike through the mall section. It's the law and it's safer! Enjoy the people-watching!

### Segment 6: 18th Street (Southbound)
- **Distance:** 249 m
- **Time:** ~39 sec
- **Direction:** Turn south onto 18th Street
- **Notes:** Short stretch between Pearl and Canyon.

### Segment 7: Canyon Boulevard (Westbound)
- **Distance:** 637 m
- **Time:** ~1 min
- **Direction:** Turn west onto Canyon Boulevard
- **Notes:** This is part of the Boulder Creek Path — a dedicated multi-use trail. Smooth pavement, separated from road traffic. The most scenic and enjoyable part of the ride!

## Seasonal Considerations

| Season | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Spring** | Great riding weather; canals and creeks are full |
| **Summer** | Early mornings are best (heat + UV). Afternoon storms — carry rain gear. |
| **Fall** | Fantastic fall colors along the Creek Path |
| **Winter** | Creek Path may accumulate ice; choose 19th Street if conditions are bad. Studded tires recommended. |

---

## Bike Maintenance Tips for Boulder

- **Check tires daily** — the chalky Colorado clay can cause flats
- **Lube your chain** — dry climate wears lubrication fast
- **Carry a repair kit** — summer rides along the Creek Path are remote from help
- **Use lights** — Boulder law requires a front light and rear reflector at night
- **Don't forget your helmet!** — Colorado law requires helmets for riders under 18; highly recommended for all ages
