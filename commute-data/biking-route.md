## Detailed Segment Analysis

### Segment 1: Oak Avenue (97 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat, residential
- **Bike Infrastructure:** No dedicated lane; low traffic
- **Safety:** Excellent; ride at walking pace
- **Notes:** Short residential stretch — easy start to the ride

### Segment 2: 19th Street (2.157 km) — 📌 Main Ride
- **Terrain:** Flat to slight downhill
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Dedicated bike-pedestrian shared path on north side
- **Traffic:** Moderate; shared path reduces conflict
- **Safety:** Very good on the shared path; watch for pedestrians at crossings
- **Notes:** This is the backbone of the bike commute. The dedicated path makes it comfortable and safe. Most Boulder cyclists use this route daily.

### Segment 3: 20th St. (106 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Sidewalk/path
- **Safety:** Low-speed residential

### Segment 4: 20th Street (566 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Residential street
- **Safety:** Low traffic; standard ride

### Segment 5: Pearl Street (216 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Shoulder riding; pedestrian-heavy
- **Traffic:** High foot traffic during business hours
- **Safety:** Dismount or walk bike through the mall section if preferred
- **Notes:** This is the trickiest segment. The Pearl Street Mall is pedestrian-focused. Ride slowly, signal, and be prepared to stop.

### Segment 6: 18th Street (249 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Downtown street
- **Traffic:** Moderate; stay visible and predictable
- **Notes:** Short stretch through downtown — for maximum visibility, take the lane if needed

### Segment 7: Canyon Boulevard (637 m) — 🌟 The Best Part!
- **Terrain:** Flat, riverside
- **Bike Infrastructure:** Protected Boulder Creek Path
- **Traffic:** Very low; separate from car traffic
- **Safety:** Excellent — this is Boulder's crown jewel for cycling
- **Notes:** The Boulder Creek Path is a paved, protected multi-use trail. It's flat, scenic, and separated from vehicles. If this were a standalone bike route, it would be perfect. End this segment with a smile! 🚲

## Seasonal Considerations for Biking

| Season | Conditions | Tips |
|--------|-----------|------|
| **Spring** | Mild, occasional rain | Fenders for road spray; light layers |
| **Summer** | Warm, afternoon thunderstorms | Early departure recommended; rain gear in bag |
| **Fall** | Cool and crisp | Ideal biking weather! Light jacket |
| **Winter** | Cold, snow/ice possible | Studded tires, lights, full fenders; consider indoor bike parking |

## Gear Recommendations

| Item | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| **Lock** | U-lock (Kryptonite or Abus) + cable for wheels |
| **Lights** | Front: 500+ lumens; Rear: blinking red |
| **Helmet** | Always! MIPS-rated recommended |
| **Fenders** | Full coverage for year-round riding |
| **Bag** | Handlebar bag or frame bag for essentials |
| **Tires** | 28–35mm commuter tires; slightly lower PSI at altitude |
| **E-bike option** | Consider Pedego or similar for zero-effort commuting |
