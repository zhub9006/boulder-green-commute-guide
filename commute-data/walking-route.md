## Detailed Segment Analysis

### Segment 1: Oak Avenue (97 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat, residential
- **Surface:** Sidewalk available
- **Traffic:** Very low — quiet morning and evening streets
- **Safety:** Excellent; well-maintained sidewalk

### Segment 2: 19th Street (2.157 km) — 📌 longest segment
- **Terrain:** Flat to slight grade
- **Surface:** Bike-pedestrian shared path available
- **Traffic:** Moderate arterial road
- **Safety:** Use the dedicated shared-use path on the north side; stay alert at crossings
- **Notes:** This is the main stretch of the commute. The path is well-maintained and popular with commuters.

### Segment 3: 20th St. (106 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Surface:** Sidewalk/path
- **Traffic:** Low residential street

### Segment 4: 20th Street (566 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Surface:** Sidewalk
- **Traffic:** Residential area; low volume

### Segment 5: Pearl Street (216 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Surface:** Sidewalk; pedestrian-heavy area
- **Traffic:** High foot traffic during business hours
- **Notes:** Historic Pearl Street Mall — enjoy the vibe, but watch for pedestrians!

### Segment 6: 18th Street (249 m)
- **Terrain:** Flat
- **Surface:** Sidewalk
- **Traffic:** Moderate downtown area

### Segment 7: Canyon Boulevard (637 m) — 🌟 scenic highlight
- **Terrain:** Flat, river buffer
- **Surface:** Paved bike path
- **Traffic:** Very low; dedicated Boulder Creek Path
- **Notes:** Beautiful stretch along Boulder Creek. Look for wildlife including herons, beavers, and turtles. This is the most enjoyable part of the commute!
