# Sustainable Commute Tips for Boulder, CO

## 🌱 Why Choose a Green Commute?

Boulder consistently ranks as one of the most environmentally conscious cities in Colorado and the nation. Choosing to walk or bike — even just 1–2 days per week — for your daily commute makes a meaningful difference.

## 📊 Quick Impact Facts

- **Boulder's transportation sector** accounts for approximately **36%** of community greenhouse gas emissions
- The average Boulder commuter drives **~40 miles per day** for all trips
- **Switching one daily commute trip** from driving to walking or biking saves ~0.8 kg of CO₂ per trip
- **Over a year** (250 round trips): you'll save ~200 kg of CO₂, ~2,507 car-miles, and $2,060–$3,760

## 🚶‍♂️ Walking Tips

### Getting Started
- **Start small:** Try walking 1–2 days per week and gradually increase
- **Wear comfortable shoes:** Invest in a good pair of walking shoes with proper arch support
- **Layer up:** Boulder's elevation (~5,400 ft) means cooler temperatures — even in summer
- **Stay hydrated:** The dry climate at this elevation increases dehydration risk
- **Use crosswalks:** Boulder has signalized crossings on major corridors — always use them

### Best Routes for Walking in Boulder
| Route | Distance | Est. Time | Notes |

| Creek Path (Downtown to Ullr Lodge) | 4.5 km | 60 min | Scenic, follows Boulder Creek |
| University Hill | 1.5 km | 20 min | Steep but rewarding views |

### Hydration & Elevation
At 5,400 ft elevation:
- You lose moisture faster through respiration
- Carry a water bottle — even for short walks
- Allow 1–2 days for acclimation if new to the area

## 🚴‍♂️ Biking Tips

### Getting Started
- **Safety first:** Always wear a helmet. Colorado law requires helmets for riders under 18, but it's a best practice for everyone
- **Lights:** Front and rear lights are essential for early morning or evening commutes
- **Lock your bike:** Use a U-lock or heavy-duty chain lock
- **Check the weather:** Boulder's afternoon thunderstorms (Jun–Aug) can make biking hazardous

## 🔄 Hybrid Commute Options

Not ready for a full walk or bike commute? Consider:

| Option | Description | Savings |
|---|---|---|
| **Walk 1 day, drive 4** | Walk just one leg of your commute per week | ~40 kg CO₂/year |
| **BCycle + RTD** | Bike to transit, ride transit, bike from transit | ~0.5 kg CO₂/trip |
| **Park & Pedal** | Park on the edge of downtown, walk the last 1–2 km | Reduces parking stress |
| **Walking meetings** | Turn short meetings into walking meetings | Health + zero emissions |
| **Bike commuting 2 days/week** | Bike every other day, drive the rest | ~80 kg CO₂/year |

| Season | Walking | Biking | Key Considerations |

| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | ⚠️ Hot midday | ✅ Great (early) | **Thunderstorms** are common — avoid biking 1–3 PM. Carry water. |
| **Fall (Sep–Nov)** | ✅ Ideal | ✅ Ideal | Best biking weather. Cool, crisp air. Fall foliage on 19th St. |
| **Winter (Dec–Feb)** | ✅ Good (cleared) | ⚠️ Challenging | Ice and snow common. Studded bike tires recommended. RTD offers bike racks on buses. |

- **Monthly review:** Check your monthly stats and set improvement goals
