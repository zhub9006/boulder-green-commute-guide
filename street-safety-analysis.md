| Safety Factor | Walking | Biking |

| **High-risk segments** | 19th Street | 19th Street (c), 20th St (c), Pearl St (c) |
| **Best segment** | Canyon Boulevard (5/5) | Canyon Boulevard (5/5) |
| **Dark commute risk** | Low — always sidewalks | Medium — need lights on 19th & 20th |
| **Weather fallibility** | Slip/fall on ice | Fall on ice + collision with cars |
| **Helmet required** | N/A | Colorado law <18; recommended always |
| **Overall safety rating** | 4/5 | 5/5 |

### Why Biking Scores Higher on Safety for This Route
The bike route uses separate path on Canyon Boulevard + bike lanes on 19th Street — the same streets as walking, but with better separation from traffic. The walking route also uses these streets but brings the walker more in the "flow" of traffic (on the sidewalk vs. the bike lane which is protected).

### Key Safety Takeaway
The inherent risk on 19th Street is the bottleneck of both modes — **this is factually the same street segment** but the bike lane, even though unbuffered, offers more separation than a sidewalk at the edge of traffic.

## Recommendations

1. **Biking is safer for this route** — especially because the Canyon Boulevard path is completely separated
2. **Walking is fine but requires more vigilance on 19th Street** — always use the crosswalks, and don't text while walking through the 19th St corridor
3. **Always use a white front + red rear light when biking after dark** — Colorado law and essential for safety on 19th Street
4. **Carry a compact U-lock** — theft is possible in the downtown Boulder area; always lock to a rack (Canyon Blvd has quick-access racks near Broadway)
5. **Consider a hi-viz vest or bright jacket** — especially on 19th Street where drivers have poor sight lines
6. **Advocacy opportunity** — installing bollards between the 19th Street bike lane and the right-turning traffic would be a high-impact improvement — contact Boulder Bike Coalition (bikeboulder.org) to participate
