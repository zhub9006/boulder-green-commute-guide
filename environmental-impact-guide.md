# 🌍 Environmental Impact Deep-Dive: Green Commuting in Boulder

## Why Transportation Matters in Boulder

Transportation is one of the largest sources of greenhouse gas emissions in Boulder, accounting for approximately **40% of the city's community-wide emissions**. By choosing to walk or bike for your daily commute, you're directly contributing to Boulder's climate action goals.

## Health Co-Benefits

### Walking (45–55 min/day, 5 days/week)
- **Cardiovascular health:** Reduced risk of heart disease by ~20–30%
- **Mental health:** Reduced anxiety and depression symptoms
- **Weight management:** ~150–200 kcal burned per commute
- **Bone density:** Weight-bearing exercise strengthens bones

### Biking (10–15 min/day, 5 days/week)
- **Cardiovascular health:** Improved heart function and lower resting heart rate
- **Joint health:** Low-impact exercise, easier on knees than running
- **Weight management:** ~50–80 kcal burned per commute
- **Air quality:** Zero local emissions contribute to better breathing

### Community Health Benefits
- **Reduced air pollution:** Fewer cars means less NOx, PM2.5, and ozone precursors
- **Safer streets:** More pedestrians and cyclists lead to slower traffic speeds
- **Social cohesion:** Walking and biking create more livable, connected neighborhoods
- **Economic vitality:** Walkable downtowns attract more businesses and spending

## Boulder's Climate Context

### City Goals
- **Climate Action Plan:** Boulder aims to reduce community GHG emissions 80% by 2050 (from 2020 baseline)
- **Transportation target:** Increase mode share of walking, biking, and transit to 40% by 2030
- **Current mode share:** Walking ~8%, biking ~5%, transit ~5%, driving ~82%

### What Your Choice Enables
Every green commute:
- ✅ Reduces demand for parking infrastructure
- ✅ Lowers road maintenance costs (lighter vehicles = less road damage)
- ✅ Supports investment in bike lanes and pedestrian infrastructure
- ✅ Demonstrates community commitment to climate action
- ✅ Inspires neighbors to try active transportation
