# 🌉 Boulder Commuting Tips

## Getting Around Boulder

Boulder is a highly walkable and bikeable city. Here are some tips for making the most of your sustainable commute.

### 🚶‍♂️ Walking

- **Best neighborhoods for walking:** Downtown, Pearl Street Mall, University Hill
- **Average walking speed:** ~5 km/h (3.1 mph) at sea level; slower at altitude
- **Hydration:** At 5,430 ft altitude, you lose water faster — carry a bottle
- **Best seasons:** Spring through fall; winter requires ice traction cleats

### 🚴‍♂️ Biking

- **Bike lanes:** Boulder has extensive bike lane network on major streets
- **Shared-use paths:** The Boulder Creek Path runs east-west through town
- **Bike parking:** Most commercial areas have bike racks
- **E-bikes:** Popular option for hilly routes and longer commutes
- **Bike share:** B-cycle (Denver Bike Share) has stations in Boulder

### 🚌 Public Transit

- **RTD buses:** Connect Boulder to Denver and surrounding areas
- **freeMetro:** Free bus service in downtown Boulder
- **HU bus:** University of Colorado bus system

### 🚗 Driving Considerations

- **Parking:** Limited and expensive downtown
- **Traffic:** Broadway and Pearl Street can be congested during rush hour
- **Alternative:** Park-and-ride at south Boulder lots + bus/downtown

## 🌤️ Seasonal Guide

| Season | Walking | Biking | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spring (Mar–May)** | ✅ Good | ✅ Excellent | Mild temps, blooming views |
| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | ✅ Good (hydrate!) | ✅ Excellent | Hot afternoons, morning commutes best |
| **Fall (Sep–Nov)** | ✅ Excellent | ✅ Excellent | Perfect conditions, stunning colors |
| **Winter (Dec–Feb)** | ⚠️ Cold/ice | ⚠️ Cold/snow | Layer up, check road conditions |

## 🏆 Boulder's Sustainability Goals

Boulder is a leader in climate action:

- **Climate Action Plan:** Carbon neutrality by 2050
- **Boulder Valley Transportation Plan:** Expanding bike and pedestrian infrastructure
- **B-cycle:** Regional bike share program
- **EV infrastructure:** Growing network of charging stations
