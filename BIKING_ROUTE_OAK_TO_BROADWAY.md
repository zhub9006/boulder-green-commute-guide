## Biking Safety Tips

1. **Colorado law:** Bicycles are treated as vehicles — follow all traffic laws
2. **Lights:** Front white light and rear red light required by law after dark
3. **Helmet:** Not required by law but strongly recommended
4. **Hand signals:** Use proper hand signals for turns
5. **Right of way:** Yield to pedestrians at all times
6. **Lock up:** Use a quality lock — bring your own or use BCycle

## Winter Biking Tips

Boulder winters can be challenging, but many residents bike year-round:
- Studded tires or fat bike for snow/ice
- Fenders for wet roads
- Front and rear lights (short daylight hours)
- Hand warmers and waterproof outerwear
- Dress in layers — you'll warm up once moving

---

## Weather & Seasonal Considerations

### Spring/Fall (Ideal)
- Temperatures typically 50–75°F — perfect biking weather
- Bring a light jacket or sweater for temperature changes
- Wildflower season (April–May) makes for a beautiful commute

### Summer
- Temperatures can reach 85–95°F — bring water
- Afternoon thunderstorms (June–August) — plan around weather
- Early morning rides are cooler and less crowded

### Winter
- Temperatures 20–45°F — dress in layers
- Snow and ice possible — use appropriate tires
- Shorter daylight hours — wear reflective gear and use lights
