# 💡 Sustainable Commuting Tips for Boulder Residents

## Making the Switch: Practical Advice

Transitioning from driving to walking or biking isn't just about the environment — it's about your health, your wallet, and your quality of life in Boulder. Here are practical tips to make the switch easier.

---

## 🚶 Walking Tips

### Before You Start
- **Start small** — Try walking 1–2 days per week and build up
- **Get proper shoes** — A good walking shoe with arch support is essential for a 4 km daily walk
- **Layer up** — Boulder's weather can change quickly, especially at elevation (5,430 ft / 1,655 m)
- **Carry water** — Dehydration happens faster at altitude

### During Your Walk
- **Pace yourself** — You don't need to power-walk. A comfortable 5 km/h pace is perfect
- **Stay visible** — Wear bright colors, especially in early morning or evening
- **Use crosswalks** — Boulder has well-marked crosswalks at major intersections
- **Enjoy the route** — This is your daily exercise and mental break. Notice the trees, the community, the changing seasons

### After Your Walk
- **Stretch** — A quick 5-minute stretch helps prevent soreness
- **Hydrate** — Drink water as soon as you arrive
- **Reward yourself** — Keep a habit tracker to see your streaks and progress

## 🚲 Biking Tips

### Essential Gear
- **Helmet** — Always wear one; it's the law in Colorado and it saves lives
- **Front light + rear light** — Required by Colorado law after dark
- **Lock** — A U-lock or heavy chain lock for your bike at the office
- **Bell** — For alerting pedestrians on shared paths
- **Reflective clothing** — Increases visibility, especially in low light

### Route Management
- **Scout your route** — Ride it on a weekend first to learn the traffic patterns
- **Check the weather** — Boulder's afternoon thunderstorms (June–August) can be intense
- **Plan for wind** — Boulder can be windy, especially on 19th Street and Canyon Boulevard
- **Know your alternatives** — BCycle bike-share stations are along the route if your bike needs maintenance

### Maintenance
- **Tire pressure** — Check weekly; Colorado's altitude means air pressure fluctuates
- **Brake check** — Ensure brakes work before every ride
- **Chain lubrication** — Monthly for regular riders
- **Winter prep** — Studded tires, fenders, and hand warmers for icy conditions

## 🌦️ Seasonal Strategies

### Spring (March–May)
- **Ideal biking weather** — Moderate temperatures, longer days
- **Watch for potholes** — Road repairs from winter can create hazards
- **Start early** — Schedule rides before afternoon showers

### Summer (June–August)
- **Thunderstorm season** — Ride early morning or evening to avoid storms
- **Hydration is critical** — Carry at least 1 liter of water
- **Sun protection** — Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a cap at 5,430 ft elevation
- **_POP. (Postponed On Pavement poisoning) —**Mountain pine beetle deadwood can be a fire risk near trails; stay informed

### Fall (September–November)
- **Best overall season** — Cool temperatures, stable weather, beautiful foliage
- **Visibility matters** — Shorter days mean more riding in low light; use lights
- **Layer up** — Morning and evening rides can be chilly at elevation

### Winter (December–February)
- **Walking is still great** — Just add warm layers and proper boots
- **Biking requires preparation** — Studded tires, fenders, full lights, waterproof gear
- **Consider mixed modes** — Bike part way, take transit for the rest on icy days
- **Stay visible** — Headlights, reflective gear, and bright clothing are essential
