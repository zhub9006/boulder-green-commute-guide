# Sustainable Commuting Tips for Boulder Residents

## 🚲 Why Bike or Walk?

### Environmental Impact

| Mode | CO₂ per Trip (vs. car) | Annual Impact (250 work days) |
|------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| Walking | ~0.7 kg saved | ~175 kg CO₂ saved |
| Biking | ~0.7 kg saved | ~175 kg CO₂ saved |

### Personal Benefits

- **Health:** 30+ minutes of daily walking or biking meets CDC exercise recommendations
- **Cost:** Save ~$1,000+/year on fuel, parking, and vehicle maintenance
- **Mental health:** Active commuting reduces stress and improves mood
- **Time:** Boulder traffic can make driving slower than biking for trips under 5 km

## 🛣️ Boulder's Bike Infrastructure

Boulder is one of the most bike-friendly cities in the US:

- **Over 300 miles of bike routes** including lanes, sharrows, and paths
- **Bike racks throughout downtown** — free and accessible
- **B-Cycle bike sharing** — stations across Boulder for short trips
- **Boulder Creek Path** — Scenic multi-use trail connecting north and south Boulder

## 📋 Making the Switch

### Start Small

1. **Try it once a week** — Bike or walk one day, drive the other four
2. **Test the route on a weekend** — Familiarize yourself before weekday pressure
3. **Plan for weather** — Keep rain gear and layers at work
4. **Pack smart** — Pannier bags or backpack for work essentials

### Make it a Habit

1. **Set a routine** — Same days each week builds consistency
2. **Find a comm buddy** — Motivation is contagious
3. **Track your progress** — Use apps like Strava or mapmyrun
4. **Reward yourself** — Save the gas money and treat yourself monthly

## 🚗 When Driving Makes Sense

Not every trip is suitable for walking or biking:

- **Bad weather** — Ice, heavy snow, thunderstorms
- **Time constraints** — When you're truly pressed for time
- **Carrying heavy items** — Large equipment or supplies
- **Late evening** — When visibility is low

But even replacing **2-3 drives per week** with active transportation makes a meaningful difference.
