## Why the Big Time Difference?

The 32-minute gap comes entirely from **19th Street** (2,157 m) and **20th Street** (566 m). Here's why:

### 19th Street: The Time Maker
- **Walking:** 5 km/h pace = 25 min for 2.16 km
- **Biking:** 15 km/h pace = 3 min for 2.16 km
- **The gap is 22 minutes** — that's where the decision really matters!

**Key insight:** If you could somehow skip or shorten 19th Street, your walk would drop dramatically. Unfortunately, 19th Street is the only major north-south corridor between Oak Avenue and downtown.

### 20th Street: The Ghost Segment
- Both modes cross this residential block in under 1.5 minutes
- No significant difference between walking and biking here
- Low traffic volume makes it safe for both modes

## Key Takeaways

1. **19th Street is the bottleneck** — it's where 54% of your commute time is spent walking, and the main safety concern for biking
2. **Canyon Boulevard is the highlight** — 637 m of separated path that makes biking feel safe and scenic
3. **Both modes are flat** — 30 m net descent, making this one of Boulder's easiest green commutes
4. **Time trade-off is significant** — 32 min more walking, but walking costs nothing and provides double the exercise
5. **The hybrid approach** often wins — mix walking and biking days based on weather, energy, and schedule
