# Commute Comparison: Walking vs. Biking vs. Driving

| Metric | 🚶 Walking | 🚴 Biking | 🚗 Driving |
|--------|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Distance** | 4.0 km | 4.0 km | 4.0 km |
| **Time** | ~7 min | ~7 min | ~5 min |
| **Calories burned** | ~200–250 | ~100–150 | 0 |
| **CO₂ emissions** | 0 kg | 0 kg | ~0.8 kg/one-way, ~1.6 kg/round-trip |

## Cost Breakdown (Annual)

### Walking (No Cost)
- Clothing: ~$50/year (dedicated walking shoes)
- **Total annual cost: ~$50**

### Biking (Low Cost)
- Maintenance: ~$100/year
- Gear (lights, helmet, lock): ~$150 one-time
- Clothing: ~$75/year
- **Total annual cost: ~$275** (first year) / ~$175/year after

### Driving (High Cost)
- Gas: ~$200/year
- Parking: ~$2,600/year
- Wear & tear: ~$500/year
- Insurance allocation: ~$300/year
- **Total annual cost: ~$3,600/year**

## Decision Matrix

Choose **walking** when:
- ✅ Weather is pleasant
- ✅ You want maximum exercise benefit
- ✅ Carrying a light load
- ✅ You want a stress-free, predictable commute

Choose **biking** when:
- ✅ You want a slightly faster commute with exercise
- ✅ Carrying a heavier load (backpack, lunch bag)
- ✅ Weather is decent (not too hot/cold/wet)
- ✅ You want to avoid 19th Street traffic during rush hour

Choose **driving** when:
- ⚠️ Severe weather (lightning, ice, extreme cold)
- ⚠️ Carrying items too large for a backpack
- ⚠️ Running late and need every minute
- ⚠️ Physical limitations prevent walking/biking

## Carbon Footprint Calculation

A typical car emits about 0.404 kg CO₂ per km (for a compact sedan).

- **One-way trip:** 4.0 km × 0.404 kg/km = **1.6 kg CO₂**
- **Round trip:** 3.2 kg CO₂ per day
- **Annual (5 days/week, 50 weeks):** 3.2 × 250 = **800 kg CO₂** saved per year

Even accounting for embodied carbon in food for walking, the net savings are substantial.
