# 🌍 Environmental Impact of Green Commuting

## The Carbon Footprint of Commuting

Transportation is one of the largest sources of greenhouse gas emissions in the United States. Individual commuting choices have a measurable, cumulative impact on Boulder's air quality and global climate.

---

## What You Save by Not Driving

### Per Single Commute (4 km / 2.5 mi)

| | CO₂ | NOₓ | PM2.5 | Water Use (indirect) |
|--|-----|------|--------|---------------------|
| **Walking** | 0 kg saved | 0 g saved | 0 g saved | ~20 liters saved |
| **Biking** | 0 kg saved | 0 g saved | 0 g saved | ~20 liters saved |
| **Bus/Transit** | 0.45 kg saved | 0.2 g saved | 0.1 g saved | ~10 liters saved |

### Interpretation of "0 kg saved"
Walking and biking produce **zero tailpipe emissions** per trip. While there are embodied emissions in producing food (for walking energy) and manufacturing bicycles, these are negligible compared to car emissions.

**Study:** A typical passenger vehicle emits about **404 grams of CO₂ per mile** (EPA). On a 2.5-mile commute, that's **~1010 grams = 1.01 kg CO₂ per trip**.

## Annual Impact for a 5-Day/Week Commuter

| | Trips/Year | CO₂ Saved | Equivalent |
|--|------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Walking commute** | 250 | 253 kg | 9 trees 🌲 |
| **Biking commute** | 250 | 253 kg | 9 trees 🌲 |
| **Transit commute** | 250 | 126 kg | 4.5 trees 🌳 |

### Fun Facts
- **253 kg CO₂** = the weight of 2 adult giant pandas
- It would take a single tree **~10 years** to absorb that much CO₂
- If 100 Boulder commuters switched to biking, that's **25,300 kg CO₂ saved per year**!

---

## Additional Environmental Benefits

### Reducing Urban Heat Islands
- Cars contribute to urban heat through dark asphalt and exhaust heat
- Fewer cars = cooler streets, especially in Boulder's summer months

### Improving Air Quality
- Boulder already has some of the cleanest air in Colorado, but every car trip avoided helps
- NO₂ and particulate matter from cars aggravate respiratory conditions

### Less Road Maintenance
- Fewer car trips mean less road wear, saving the city maintenance budget
- Those funds can be redirected to bike paths and pedestrian infrastructure

### Protecting Boulder Creek
- Rain runoff from roads carries oil, heavy metals, and tire particles into Boulder Creek
- Fewer cars = less pollution washing into our beloved creek

---

## The Cumulative Effect

If Boulder's downtown workers alone (estimated 30,000 daily commuters) shifted just **10%** to active transportation:

- **3,000 commuters × 250 trips/year × 1 kg CO₂ saved = 750,000 kg CO₂ saved annually**
- That's equivalent to **taking 160 cars off the road** for a year
- Or the energy consumption of **~70 homes** for a year
