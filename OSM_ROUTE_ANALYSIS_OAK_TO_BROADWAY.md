## 🏔️ Boulder-Specific Commute Tips

### Weather Considerations
- **Summer (June–Sept):** Ideal for biking — long days, warm temps. Bring sunscreen and water.
- **Fall (Sept–Nov):** Great walking weather; biking is still pleasant with layers.
- **Winter (Dec–Feb):** Expect snow/ice on sidewalks. Use studded tires for biking; dress in layers.
- **Spring (Mar–May):** Unpredictable — carry a rain jacket. Trails can be muddy.

### Safety Tips
- **Use bike lights** — Boulder law requires front white and rear red lights at night
- **Signal your turns** — Hand signals are both the law and courtesy
- **Be alert on 19th Street** — busiest corridor; watch for distracted drivers
- **Canyon Boulevard bike path** is separated from traffic — enjoy the ride!
- **Pedestrian right-of-way** is respected at most Boulder intersections

### Gear Recommendations
- **Walkers:** Comfortable shoes, weather-appropriate layers, reusable water bottle
- **Cyclists:** Quality helmet, front/rear lights, lock for downtown parking, pannier or backpack
- **Both:** UV protection (Boulder's altitude = stronger sun at ~5,400 ft)

### Nearby Amenities
- **Coffee shops** along Pearl Street and 19th Street for pre-commute fuel
- **Bike repair stations** near downtown Canyon Boulevard
- **Bus stops** — CBXM bus routes parallel this corridor for multi-modal options
- **Shower facilities** — Check with your office building for post-ride amenities
