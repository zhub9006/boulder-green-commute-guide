# Sustainable Travel Tips for Boulder Commuters

## 🚶 Walking Best Practices

1. **Stay on designated paths** — Use boulder's extensive sidewalk network
2. **Watch for roosters** — Downtown Boulder is famous for its street ducks and wildlife
3. **Carry water** — Boulder's altitude (5,430 ft) dehydrates you faster
4. **Sun protection** — High altitude means stronger UV; wear sunscreen & hat
5. **Layer clothing** — Temperature can swing 20°F from morning to midday

## 🚲 Biking Best Practices

1. **Use Canyon Boulevard** — Boulder's premier multi-use path, completely separated from car traffic
2. **Install lights** — Front white / rear red lights are mandatory after dark
3. **Lock properly** — Use a U-lock at Pearl Street businesses; bike racks are plentiful
4. **Check tire pressure** — Boulder's clay soils can be slick when wet
5. **Learn the sharrows** — Shared lane markings guide you through bike-ambiguous streets

## 🌍 Environmental Tips

### Reduce Your Footprint Further:
- **Combine trips** — Do errands on your commute route
- **Carpool occasionally** — Boulder West Circle Drive has Park-n-Ride lots
- **Use REI or BOBS** — Bike maintenance stations at both Pearl Street locations
- **Seasonal transitions** — Switch to studded tires Nov-Feb for icy 13th Street hills

### What NOT to Do:
- ❌ Don't bike on Pearl Street sidewalk (pedestrian priority zone)
- ❌ Don't walk in bike lanes (dangerous at speed)
- ❌ Don't leave electronics in parked cars (Boulder has high auto-theft)
- ❌ Don't rely on gas generators for e-bike charging (use grid/Terrapower)
