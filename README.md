## 🚶 Walking Route Summary

- **Total Distance:** 4.03 km (~2.5 miles)
- **Estimated Time:** ~50–55 minutes
- **Terrain:** Mostly flat, urban streets with sidewalks
- **Key Streets:** Oak Avenue → 19th Street → 20th Street → Pearl Street → 18th Street → Canyon Boulevard
- **Best For:** No equipment needed, maximum exercise, zero emissions
- **Step Count:** ~5,000–6,000 steps one way

## 🚲 Biking Route Summary

- **Total Distance:** 4.03 km (~2.5 miles)
- **Estimated Time:** ~15–20 minutes (about 3x faster than walking!)
- **Terrain:** Mostly flat, urban streets with bike lane infrastructure
- **Key Streets:** Same as walking route, with bike lanes on 19th St and Canyon Blvd
- **Important:** Must dismount and walk your bike through the Pearl Street Mall pedestrian zone

## 🌍 Sustainability Impact

Boulder is committed to reducing greenhouse gas emissions under its [Climate Action Plan](https://bouldercolorado.gov/climate-action-plan). Transportation is the **largest source** of emissions in the city. By choosing to walk or bike just 2–3 days per week on this route, you can:

- Reduce your personal transportation emissions by 20–30%
- Contribute to Boulder's goal of 80% emission reduction by 2050
- Improve your cardiovascular health and mental well-being
- Reduce traffic congestion on key corridors like 19th Street
- Save money on gas, parking, and vehicle maintenance
