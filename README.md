## Tips for Boulder Commuters

### 🚶 Walking Tips
- **Best for**: Short-distance commuters living within 2–3 miles of work
- **Gear**: Comfortable walking shoes; packable rain shell (Boulder gets ~30" precipitation/year)
- **Safety**: Use marked crosswalks on 19th Street — the busiest segment
- **Footwear**: Invest in comfortable walking shoes with good cushioning
- **Timing**: Allow 10–15 extra minutes during winter for icy sidewalks
- **Step count**: ~6,000 steps one way — great for midday energy!
- **Mindfulness**: Use the 50-minute walk for podcasts, audiobooks, or contemplation

### 🚲 Biking Tips
- **Best for**: Commuters who want speed without driving costs
- **Infrastructure**: Boulder is a Platinum-level Bicycle Friendly Community
- **Gear**: Colorado law requires front white and rear red lights at night (Boulder Law §10-4-2)
- **Bike lanes**: Use designated bike lanes on 19th Street; dismount on Pearl Street Mall
- **Parking**: Secure both frame and rear wheel with a U-lock at 1800 Broadway
- **Winter riding**: Studded tires recommended Nov–Feb; major bike lanes are plowed
- **B-Cycle**: Consider bike-sharing for zero-impact commuting (~$9/day)
