## 💡 Tips for Sustainable Commuting in Boulder

### Walking Tips
1. **Layer up**: Boulder's weather can swing 20°F+ between morning and afternoon — dress in removable layers.
2. **Use the Pearl Street Mall**: The pedestrian-priority zone through downtown is safe and pleasant.
3. **Carry a rain shell**: Boulder averages ~30 inches of precipitation per year — a lightweight shell keeps you comfortable.
4. **Plan around peak foot traffic**: Downtown is busiest 7-9 AM and 4-6 PM; walking slightly earlier or later can be more pleasant.

### Biking Tips
1. **Use the Boulder Creek Path**: This shared-use path connects many neighborhoods and is a scenic alternative to street biking.
2. **Check bike lane maps**: The City of Boulder maintains an interactive bike lane map — plan your route to use protected lanes where available.
3. **Secure parking**: 1800 Broadway area has bike racks; bring a quality U-lock and always lock both frame and wheels.
4. **Gear up**: A helmet is recommended; lights and reflectors are essential for early-morning or evening commutes.
5. **Flatirons weather watch**: Wind gusts can be significant along Canyon Boulevard — lower your gear and stay alert.

### General Sustainability Tips
- **Combine trips**: Walk or bike for errands near your office to maximize your green commute impact.
- **Transit backup**: RTD buses serve the Boulder area — use them as a backup on severe weather days.
- **Carpool for days you can't walk/bike**: If driving is unavoidable, carpooling cuts per-person emissions by ~50%.
