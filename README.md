# 🌿 Boulder Green Commute Guide

A practical guide for Boulder residents exploring sustainable transportation options. This guide provides detailed walking and biking route analyses with real-world travel times to help you reduce your carbon footprint, save money, and stay healthy.

## Why Go Green?

| Mode | CO₂ per km (avg) | Cost per km | Health Benefit |
|------|-------------------|-------------|----------------|
| 🚗 Car | ~271g | ~$0.60+ | None |
| 🚲 Bike | ~0g | ~$0.05 | Cardio, strength |

Choosing to walk or bike instead of driving just **one round-trip per day** can save you over **$1,000 per year** and prevent roughly **1.5 metric tons of CO₂ annually**.

## 🚶 Walking Route

| Metric | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Distance** | 4.0 km (2.5 miles) |
| **Estimated Time** | 48–55 minutes |
| **Calories Burned** | ~280–350 kcal |
| **Difficulty** | Easy — mostly flat, paved sidewalks |

### Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Head south on Oak Avenue** — 100m (~1.2 min)
   - Leave your home heading south on Oak Avenue. Sidewalks are well-maintained.

2. **Turn right onto 19th Street** — 2.2 km (~26 min)
   - This is the longest segment. The street has dedicated sidewalks and light traffic on weekends.
   - You'll pass through a residential area with tree-lined streets.

3. **Brief segment along 20th Street (west-side connector)** — 106m (~1.3 min)
   - Quick connector toward downtown.

4. **Continue along 20th Street southbound** — 566m (~6.8 min)
   - Continue south; you'll approach the downtown area.

5. **Turn left onto Pearl Street** — 216m (~2.6 min)
   - Pearl Street is the heart of downtown Boulder. Enjoy the pedestrian-friendly atmosphere, street performers, and local shops.

6. **Turn right onto 18th Street eastbound** — 249m (~3.0 min)
   - Short segment heading toward Canyon Boulevard.

7. **Turn right onto Canyon Boulevard** — 637m (~7.6 min)
   - This is the final stretch. Canyon Boulevard has a wide multi-use path alongside the road. You'll arrive at your destination at 1800 Broadway.

### Walking Tips

- 🕐 **Peak hours**: Avoid 7:00–8:30 AM if possible; 19th Street can be busy.
- 🌧️ **Rainy days**: Most of the route has sidewalk coverage except portions of 19th Street.
- 🎧 **Safety**: Keep headphones low — Canyon Boulevard is a busy corridor.
- 💧 **Hydration**: Carry a bottle; Boulder's elevation (~5,430 ft) means you dehydrate faster!

## 🚲 Biking Route

| Metric | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Distance** | 4.0 km (2.5 miles) |
| **Estimated Time** | 15–20 minutes |
| **Calories Burned** | ~80–100 kcal |
| **Difficulty** | Easy — flat terrain, bike lanes on most segments |
| **E-Gain** | ~30m (~100 ft) — very gentle grade |

### Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Head south on Oak Avenue** — 100m (~30 sec)
   - Leave home heading south. Oak Avenue has quiet residential traffic; ride in the bike lane if available.

2. **Continue south on 19th Street** — 2.2 km (~8.5 min)
   - 19th Street has a painted bike lane. This is the longest segment — enjoy the peaceful residential stretch.

3. **Brief connector via 20th Street** — 106m (~30 sec)
   - Quick transition toward downtown.

4. **Continue along 20th Street southbound** — 566m (~2.5 min)
   - Moderate traffic; stay in the bike lane.

5. **Turn left onto Pearl Street** — 216m (~50 sec)
   - Pearl Street is pedestrian-heavy in the bandshell area. Dismount if needed in crowded zones.

6. **Turn right onto 18th Street** — 249m (~1 min)
   - Short segment with moderate bike traffic.

7. **Turn right onto Canyon Boulevard (SH 119)** — 637m (~2.5 min)
   - **Key segment**: Canyon Boulevard has a **dedicated multi-use path** (the **Boulder Creek Path**) alongside the road. This is the most scenic and safest portion of the ride.
   - Follow the path along Canyon Creek — you may see herons, turtles, and creek-side greenery!

### Biking Tips

- 🚦 **Canyon Boulevard**: Use the multi-use path when available; it's separated from vehicle traffic.
- 🔒 **Parking**: 1800 Broadway has bike racks — lock your bike securely.
- 🌨️ **Winter**: Boulder receives ~90" of snow annually. Consider studded tires and a winter jacket for Nov–Mar.
- 🛡️ **Safety first**: A front light (even during daytime in Canyon Boulevard's shaded sections) and a bell are recommended.
- ⚡ **E-bikes**: A pedal-assist e-bike makes this commute effortless — many Boulder residents use them!

## 🌱 Make It a Habit

1. **Start small** — Try biking or walking just **2 days per week**.
2. **Pack smart** — Use a backpack or pannier. Boulder's Cyclery and REI offer great commuter gear.
3. **Track your impact** — Use apps like **JouleBug**, **Oroeco**, or **Litterati** to quantify your carbon savings.
4. **Find a buddy** — Boulder has several community bike groups. Join a ride to stay motivated!

### Boulder-Specific Perks

- 🏔️ **Flatfish Corridor**: Your route connects to the Flatiron Loop — explore the famous rock formations on weekends!
- 💧 **Boulder Creek Path**: The path along Canyon Boulevard is one of Colorado's best urban greenways.
- ☀️ **300+ sunny days/year**: Boulder's climate is ideal for year-round walking and biking.
- 🛒 **Drop-in downtown**: Need groceries? Pearl Street has a entire block of shops in 2 city blocks — easy to run errands on foot.
