# 🚶‍♂️🚲 Boulder Green Commute Guide

A comprehensive guide for Boulder residents to make sustainable travel choices with detailed walking and biking commute analyses, route details, and travel times.

## 🌿 About This Project

This guide helps Boulder residents explore greener commuting options by providing data-driven walking and biking route analyses. Every green commute makes a difference for Boulder's air quality, health, and climate goals!

**Boulder's Climate Goal:** 80% emission reduction by 2050 — every walk, bike, or bus ride gets us closer.

## 🌎 Why Green Commuting Matters for Boulder

### Environmental Impact
- The average car commute on this route produces **~0.8 kg of CO₂ per trip**
- Switching to biking or walking eliminates this entirely
- **5 days/week saves ~200 kg CO₂ per year** — equivalent to 9 trees' worth of annual carbon absorption!

### Health Benefits
- **Walking 40+ min/day** reduces risk of heart disease, diabetes, and obesity
- **Biking** is low-impact cardio — easier on joints than running
- Both options boost mental health and reduce stress
- At 5,430 ft elevation, your body works 10-15% harder — great for fitness!

### Economic Benefits
- Save **~$1,000+ per month** on gas, parking, and car maintenance
- No insurance, registration, or depreciation costs
- Boulder's free bike-share program (B-cycle) makes cycling even more accessible

## 🚴 Boulder Commuting Tips

### For Walkers
1. **Start early** — Daylight hours in winter are limited; plan accordingly
2. **Layer up** — Boulder mornings can be 15-20°F colder than afternoons
3. **Use Canyon Boulevard** — The dedicated separated path offers a scenic, car-free finish
4. **Weekend exploration** — Try walking the route on weekends to explore Pearl Street Mall

### For Cyclists
1. **Use bike lanes** — 19th Street has a painted bike lane; ride confidently in it
2. **Dismount on Pearl** — The pedestrian mall prohibits biking; walk your bike through
3. **Canyon Blvd is the reward** — 636.6 m of separated bike lane — the safest segment!
4. **Winter riding** — Fenders, lights, and reflective gear are essential Nov–Mar
5. **B-cycle** — Use Boulder's bike-share system for quick trips without bringing your own bike!

## 📈 Making the Switch

### Start Small
- **Try 1 day/week** — Bike or walk to work once a week
- **Combo commute** — Bike to the trailhead, park, and walk
- **WFH + active** — Use commute time for a morning walk or ride

### Track Your Progress
- Use Strava, MapMyRun, or the City of Boulder Green Trip counter
- Join the **Boulder Move More Challenge** for incentives
- Compare your carbon savings at [CO₂ calculators](https://www.epa.gov/energy/greenhouse-gas-equiv-computer-calc)
