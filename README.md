# Boulder Green Commute Guide 🌿🚲🚶

*A sustainable commute analysis guide for Boulder, Colorado residents — featuring walking and biking route details, travel times, and tips for greener transportation choices.*

## About This Guide

This repository contains a detailed analysis of green commute options for Boulder residents transitioning from car-based travel to walking and biking. Every route analyzed uses OpenStreetMap data and real-world routing to provide accurate travel times and directions.

## Route Overview

Both walking and biking routes follow the same corridor through central Boulder:

1. **Oak Avenue** — Start from your home on Oak Avenue in the 80304 zip code area
2. **19th Street** — Head south through the residential neighborhood
3. **20th Street** — Continue south, passing through a mix of residential and commercial areas
4. **Pearl Street** — Enter the vibrant Pearl Street Mall area (bike-friendly with dedicated lanes)
5. **18th Street** — Navigate through the downtown core
6. **Canyon Boulevard** — Final stretch heading toward Broadway and your office at 1800 Broadway (80302)

## Walking Route Details 🚶

- **Total Distance:** 4.03 km (~2.5 miles)
- **Estimated Time:** ~50 minutes at a comfortable walking pace (5 km/h)
- **Terrain:** Mostly flat with gentle grades through Boulder's core
- **Surface:** Primarily paved sidewalks and shared-use paths
- **Key Highlights:**
  - Passes through the Pearl Street Mall — a pedestrian-friendly zone with shops and cafes
  - Uses Boulder's extensive sidewalk network
  - Well-lit and populated during daytime hours
  - Connects to multiple bus routes for multi-modal options

## Biking Route Details 🚲

- **Total Distance:** 4.03 km (~2.5 miles)
- **Estimated Time:** ~15 minutes at a moderate cycling pace (16 km/h)
- **Terrain:** Mostly flat; Boulder's elevation is ~5,430 ft with minimal grade changes on this route
- **Surface:** Paved roads with bike lanes and sharrows in key sections
- **Key Highlights:**
  - Dedicated bike lanes on several segments
  - Pearl Street Mall has bike racks and infrastructure
  - Canyon Boulevard offers wide shoulders for cycling
  - Boulder's bike-forward infrastructure makes this a safe commute

## Boulder Green Commute Tips

- 🌅 **Start Early:** Summer mornings in Boulder are beautiful and less crowded on bike paths
- 🎒 **Pack Light:** A good commuting backpack or pannier makes a huge difference
- 🔒 **Security:** Use a quality U-lock for bike parking; downtown Boulder has secure bike racks
- 🌧️ **Weather Prep:** Boulder's weather can change quickly — keep rain gear at work
- 🚌 **Multi-Modal:** Combine walking/biking with RTD bus service for longer distances
- 👥 **Buddy Up:** Find a commute partner for safety and motivation
- 📱 **Apps:** Use Strava, Komoot, or Boulder's own bike map resources for route planning
