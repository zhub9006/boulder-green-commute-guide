## 🌍 Environmental Impact

By choosing to walk or bike instead of driving, you can save approximately:

- **~8 kg CO₂ per day** (based on average car emissions for 4 km)
- **~2,400 kg CO₂ per year** (assuming ~300 commute days)
- **Fuel savings** of ~$500–$800 annually (at current gas prices)
- **Reduced traffic congestion** and parking demand in downtown Boulder

## 🗺️ Alternative Sustainable Options

While this guide focuses on walking and biking, Boulder also offers:

- **RTD Bus Service** — several routes connect the west side of Boulder to downtown
- **Boulder B-Cycle** — bike-sharing stations throughout the city
- **Carpooling / Vanpooling** — reduce emissions by sharing rides
- **Electric scooters** — available from several rental companies in Boulder

## 📋 Tips for Boulder Commuters

1. **Plan your route** — Use this guide and OSM routing tools to find the best path
2. **Check the weather** — Boulder weather can change quickly; layer up or carry rain gear
3. **Prepare the night before** — Lay out your walking/biking clothes and pack your bag
4. **Stay safe** — Use lights, wear reflective gear, and follow traffic signals
5. **Track your progress** — Use apps like Strava or Hiking Project to log your green commutes
6. **Join a community** — Connect with other Boulder commuters at bike.library.colorado.edu or local cycling groups
