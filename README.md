## 💡 Why Go Green in Boulder?

### 🌍 Environmental Benefits
- **CO₂ saved per trip:** ~1.5 kg (vs. driving ~2.5 miles)
- **CO₂ saved per year (250 workdays):** ~375 kg
- **Fuel saved per year:** ~125 liters (~33 gallons)

### 💰 Financial Benefits
- **Annual savings vs. driving:** ~$1,500–$3,000 (fuel, maintenance, parking, insurance)
- **No parking fees downtown**
- **No car payment needed**

### 🏃‍♂️ Health Benefits
- Walking: ~240 calories burned per commute (round-trip = ~480)
- Biking: ~300 calories burned per commute (round-trip = ~600)
- Improved cardiovascular health, reduced stress, better sleep

### ⏱️ Time Comparison
| Mode | One Way | Round Trip |

| 🚗 Driving (with traffic/searching for parking) | 10–15 min | 25–40 min |
| 🚶 Walking | ~48 min | ~96 min |
| 🚲 Biking | ~16 min | ~32 min |

> **Pro tip:** Biking is only ~3 minutes slower than driving door-to-door in Boulder, and you get exercise and zero parking stress!

## 🚏 Boulder-Specific Commuting Tips

1. **Boulder Creek Path** – Use this paved multi-use trail for a scenic, car-free ride along Boulder Creek. It's separated from road traffic.
2. **Bike joke tellers (Boulder's traffic calming)** – Slow down and enjoy the neighborhood feel; these make biking safer.
3. **Downtown parking** – If you do drive occasionally, use the City Center parking structure (~$1/hr) or the 13th Street garage.
4. **B-Cycle bike share** – Boulder has a public bike-share system; no need to own a bike if you want to try it out!
5. **Weather prep** – Boulder is sunny 300+ days a year, but carry a light rain layer and dress in layers for temperature swings.
6. **Walk-bike combo** – When weather is bad, consider walking part of the route and using a bike for the flatter sections.
