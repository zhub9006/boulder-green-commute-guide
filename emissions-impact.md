## Boulder's Air Quality Context

Boulder's Mile-High location and persistent winter inversions can trap pollutants. Transportation is the **#1 source** of local CO₂ emissions. Every green commute counts:

- 🌬️ **Ozone season** (May–Sept) — driving reduction is critical
- ❄️ **Winter inversions** (Dec–Feb) — low-emission vehicles & alternatives essential
- 🏔️ **Brown cloud** — Boulder-Denver corridor pollution concentrates in foothills

## How to Offset Your Remaining Emissions

Even a mixed-mode commute (car 3 days, bike 2 days) makes a difference:

1. **Direct reduction** — Switch 1 car trip/week = 78 kg CO₂ saved/year
2. **Carbon credits** — Boulder offsets via [Climate Action Plan tax](https://bouldercolorado.gov/climate-action-plan)
3. **Local trees** — Plant in Boulder's [Greenbase program](https://bouldercolorado.gov/urban-forestry)
4. **Advocate** — Support expanded bike lanes on Arapahoe & 28th

### Boulder's Climate Goal

> *City of Boulder targets **80% reduction in community GHG emissions by 2050** (from 2005 baseline). Transportation electrification + mode shift account for 35% of needed reductions.*
