# Multi-Modal & Transit Options

> **Route:** 45 Oak Avenue ↔ 1800 Broadway, Boulder, CO
> **Last updated:** 2025

---

## 🚌 RTD Bus Options

Boulder's **RTD (Regional Transportation District)** bus system serves the corridor between Oak Avenue and Broadway. Several routes run along 19th Street and Broadway, making multi-modal commuting easy.

### Option 1: Walk + Bus
1. **Walk** from 45 Oak Avenue to the **19th & Canyon bus stop** (~8 min walk)
2. **Board** RTD Route 6 (19th–Broadway corridor) heading south
3. **Alight** at **Broadway & 18th** stop (~5 min ride)
4. **Walk** east to 1800 Broadway (~2 min)

**Total time:** ~20–25 minutes (including wait time)
**Cost:** $2.60 per ride (RTD local fare, as of 2025)
**CO₂ saved vs. driving:** ~0.8 kg per trip

### Option 2: Bike + Bus (Bike-and-Ride)
1. **Bike** from Oak Avenue to the **19th & Canyon bike-and-ride facility** (~8 min)
2. **Lock your bike** at the RTD Bike-and-Ride rack (free, no reservation needed)
3. **Board** RTD Route 6 southbound
4. **Alight** at Broadway & 18th
5. **Walk** to office (~2 min)

**Total time:** ~20–25 minutes
**Cost:** $2.60 bus fare (bike is free!)
**CO₂ saved:** ~0.8 kg per trip

### Option 3: BCycle + Walk
1. **Walk** to the nearest **BCycle Boulder station** (~5 min from Oak Ave)
2. **Check out** a BCycle e-bike ($9/day pass)
3. **Ride** south on the Canyon Boulevard bike path
4. **Return** the bike at the **Broadway & 13th BCycle station**
5. **Walk** the remaining blocks to 1800 Broadway

**Total time:** ~20–25 minutes
**Cost:** $9/day (BCycle day pass)
**CO₂ saved:** ~0.8 kg per trip

### Option 4: Walk + BCycle Hybrid
1. **Walk** from Oak Avenue to downtown (~25 min)
2. **BCycle** the last few blocks if needed (~5 min)
3. **Walk** from BCycle return point to office

**Total time:** ~30–35 minutes
**Cost:** $9/day (BCycle)
**Best for:** Days you want exercise but don't want to carry a bike through the mall

## 💡 Multi-Modal Tips

- **RTD EcoPass:** Ask your employer if they participate in the RTD EcoPass program — many Boulder companies subsidize transit passes
- **Bike-and-Ride:** All RTD bikes-and-rides are free and first-come-first-served — no need to book
- **BCycle apps:** Download the BCycle app for real-time bike availability and station locations
- **RTD app:** Use the RTD app for real-time bus arrivals and route planning
- **Combined trips:** On wet mornings, bike to the bus stop and take the bus — keep your bike dry and still get exercise
- **Off-peak fares:** RTD has reduced fares during off-peak hours — if you can shift your commute slightly, you may save money
- **Student discounts:** RTD offers discounted passes for students — check eligibility
