# 🚀 Getting Started: Eco-Friendly Commuting Basics

## Why Commute Green?

Boulder is one of the most eco-friendly cities in the United States. By choosing to walk, bike, or take public transit, you're contributing to:

- **A cleaner environment** — Boulder's air quality rankings consistently rank among the best in the nation
- **A healthier you** — Active commuting reduces heart disease risk, improves mood, and boosts energy
- **A quieter city** — Fewer cars mean less noise pollution for everyone
- **A stronger community** — Walking and biking help you know your neighbors and see the city's character

## 🚲 Bike Rental: Boulder B-Cycle

**Boulder B-Cycle** is the city's bike-share program, perfect if you don't own a bike or want a backup option.

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Price** | $1/ride (30 min) or $85/year / $15/month |
| **Stations** | ~30 stations throughout Boulder |
| **E-bike option** | Available at select stations |
| **App** | B-cycle app for finding docks and reservations |
| **Customer service** | Available for setup and support |

### How to Get Started:
1. Download the B-cycle app on your phone
2. Create an account (credit card or Apple/Google Pay)
3. Find a station near your home or office
4. Unlock a bike with the app or terminal
5. Ride to work and return to any B-cycle station

### Station Locations Near This Route:
- **Broadway & Iris** — Closest to Oak Ave origin
- **Broadway & Spruce** — Near downtown
- **Pearl Street** — Downtown corridor
- **Canyon Blvd & Broadway** — Near office destination

## 🛴 E-Bike Option

If the 48-minute walk feels like too much or the 16-minute bike ride on return is tiring, consider an **e-bike**:

- **Colorado E-Bike Rebate:** Up to $1,200 per e-bike!
  - Apply at: energystone.colorado.gov
  - Rebate covers 25-50% of e-bike cost
  - Popular e-bikes: Aventon, Trek, Specialized

- **E-bike benefits:**
  - Flatten the hills (not much here, but helpful on return)
  - Arrive at work without breaking a sweat
  - Go farther with less effort
  - Carry heavier loads (groceries, beach gear)

## 🚌 Public Transit Plus Bike

**RTD (Regional Transportation District)** serves Boulder with bus routes that have bike racks:

| Route | Coverage | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Route 1 | North Boulder to downtown | Every 15–30 min |
| Route 3 | Pearl Street to East Boulder | Every 15–30 min |
| Route 7 | Highway 7 corridor | Every 15–30 min |
| Route 10 | Boulder to Denver | Every 30 min |

### Using Bus + Bike:
1. Load your bike onto the front bus rack (driver assists)
2. Ride to your destination
3. Unload your bike when you arrive
4. Use the bike to complete your trip

RTD Bus Plus passes are available: $75/month for unlimited bus + bike rack access.

## 🏠 Essential Gear Checklist

### For Walking:
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes
- [ ] Weather-appropriate layers (300+ sunny days = big swings)
- [ ] Small backpack or messenger bag (no bulky backpacks on crowded transit)
- [ ] Water bottle
- [ ] Optional: Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat

### For Biking:
- [ ] Bike (own or B-cycle)
- [ ] Helmet (required by law in Boulder for under 18, recommended for all ages)
- [ ] Front headlight (white) and rear tail-light (red) — required if riding after sunset
- [ ] Bell (required by law)
- [ ] Optional: Pannier or backpack for carrying work clothes, lunch, laptop
- [ ] Optional: Fenders (for rainy days), mudguards (winter)

### Office Setup (Bike Commuters):
- [ ] Bike rack or secure parking (1800 Broadway has bike parking)
- [ ] City locker or secure storage for valuables
- [ ] Shower/locker access (check with your building)
- [ ] Fresh clothes at the office or shower nearby
