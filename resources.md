# 🚌 Boulder Transit & Green Travel Resources

## RTD Bus

### Route 201 / 202 — Regional Transportation District
- **Corridor:** Serves the Arapahoe Avenue and Broadway corridor
- **Nearest stops to 45 Oak Avenue:** Bus stop at Arapahoe & Folsom (0.3 mi)
- **Nearest stops to 1800 Broadway:** Multiple stops on Broadway in downtown Boulder
- **Frequency:** Every 10–15 minutes peak hours
- **Fare:** $2.50/ride or $5/day pass; monthly pass ~$75
- **Trip planning:** [rtd-denver.com](https://www.rtd-denver.com) or use the Transit App
- **Good for:** Winter weather, bad air quality days, rainy days, carrying heavy items

## B-Cycle (Bike Share)

### Boulder B-Cycle
- **Stations near start:** Several within 0.5 mile of Oak Avenue area
- **Stations near destination:** Multiple in downtown Boulder (13th & Broadway, 15th & Broadway, Pearl & 13th)
- **Cost:** $12/month for unlimited 30-minute rides; $40/3-day visitor pass
- **How it works:** Tap in/out at any station with the app or membership card
- **Great for:** One-way trips, supplementing your bike, bad weather backup
- **Info:** [b-cycle.com/boulder](https://www.b-cycle.com/boulder) or the B-Cycle app

## City of Boulder Bike Infrastructure

### Official Resources
- **Bike Trails Map:** [bouldercolorado.gov/biketrails](https://www.bouldercolorado.gov/biketrails)
- **Boulder on the Move:** [bouldercolorado.gov/bmun GOV](https://www.bouldercolorado.gov/bmud) — City transportation initiatives
- **Safe Routes to School:** Programs applicable to adult commuters too
- **Bike Registration:** Free bike registration through Boulder PD — helps recover stolen bikes

### Major Bike Routes Near This Commute
- **Canyon Boulevard (US-36 bike path):** Buffered multi-use path, separate from traffic
- **Crosstown Route:** Connects east-west neighborhoods via low-traffic streets
- **Foothills Trail:** Scenic path along the base of the Flatirons
- **CU Campus Path:** Connects north Boulder to downtown via the university

## Walk Boulder

### Pedestrian Resources
- **Boulder's Walk Score:** Downtown Boulder is very walkable (Walk Score 80+)
- **Sidewalk Coverage:** Most of this commute has sidewalks on both sides
- **Crosswalk Safety:** Use marked crosswalks; Boulder PD enforces pedestrian right-of-way on Broadway
- **Pedestrian Signal Timing:** Flash walking person on Broadway indicates you have time to cross

---

## Carpool & Vanpool

### If You Need to Drive Sometimes
- **Boulder Carpool Matching:** [bouldercolorado.gov/carpool](https://www.bouldercolorado.gov/carpool) — Find colleagues to share rides
- **Park & Ride:** Treat Boulevard Park & Ride off Arapahoe — drive in from the west, then bus/bike from there
- **HOV Lane:** US-36 has HOV lanes during peak hours

---

## Electric Vehicle (EV) & E-Bike

### E-Bikes in Boulder
- **Best for:** Hilly routes, extending range, arriving without breaking a sweat
- **Local shops:** Community Cyclist (2450 63rd St), Mountain Sports (2328 28th St)
- **Recommended models:** Trek Allant+, Gazelle Medeo, Specialized Turbo Vado
- **E-bike cost:** $1,500–$4,000; some Boulder employers offer e-bike purchase assistance

### Charging Infrastructure
- **For e-bikes:** Home outlet is sufficient (standard 110V); charge overnight
- **For EVs:** Boulder has excellent EV charging — see Boulder County's EV map

## Summary: Best Options by Scenario

| Scenario | Best Option |
|----------|------------|
| Good weather, time-efficient | 🚴 Bike (14–18 min) |
| Morning workout, no time pressure | 🚶 Walk (48–52 min) |
| Rain/snow, winter | 🚌 RTD Bus + walk last mile |
| Carrying heavy items | 🚶 Walk or 🚌 Bus |
| Want to arrive fresh for a presentation | 🚴 Bike or ⚡ E-Bike |
| Cheapest option | 🚶 Walk (free) |
| Most eco-friendly | 🚶 Walking or 🚴 Biking (both zero direct emissions) |
| Summer heat | 🚴 Bike early morning or 🚌 Bus |
| Social/community feel | 🚶 Walk with friends, or B-Cycle group rides |
