## 🌳 80304 — The Hill / North Boulder

### Who Lives Here?
- CU Boulder faculty, staff, and students
- Young families near Boulder Waldorf Preschool
- Retirees in quiet, tree-lined streets

### What Makes It Special
- **Tree canopy** — American Elms create a cool, shaded canopy along Oak Avenue
- **Safety** — Residential streets with low traffic volumes
- **Proximity to CU** — Walking distance to campus and Hill amenities
- **Green space** — Multiple playgrounds and pocket parks within walking distance

### What's Missing
- Limited grocery options within 1 km
- Few restaurants/cafés compared to downtown
- No dedicated bike lanes on Oak Avenue
- Limited transit service (RTD buses on 19th St, not on Oak Ave itself)

---

## 🏙️ 80302 — Downtown Boulder / Broadway

### Who Works/Lives Here?
- Office workers (courthouse, county government, private offices)
- Students and young professionals in downtown lofts
- Residents of the Hill neighborhood who work nearby
- Tourists and visitors (Pearl Street Mall)

### What Makes It Special
- **Pearl Street Mall** — Car-free pedestrian promenade with 100+ shops and restaurants
- **Bike infrastructure** — 20+ bike parking facilities within 500m of 1800 Broadway
- **Transit hub** — RTD bus stops, B-Cycle stations, and downtown transit center
- **River access** — Boulder Creek Path runs through the area
- **Cultural amenities** — University Hill Market, Galleria, and nearby CU facilities

### What's Missing
- Limited green space / parks in the immediate downtown core
- Parking is expensive and scarce (another reason to bike/walk!)
- Noise and pedestrian congestion on Pearl Street during peak hours

## 🔄 Supporting Both Neighborhoods

As a green commuter, you're the connective tissue between these two very different Boulder neighborhoods:

| Your Action | Benefits 80304 (North) | Benefits 80302 (Downtown) |
|-------------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Choose walking | Quieter streets (fewer cars) | Better air quality on Broadway |
| Choose biking | Supports bike infrastructure on 19th St | Increases demand for separated bike paths |
| Use B-Cycle | Expands bike-share demand northward | Funds station expansion in downtown |
| Walk the full route | Supports local cafés on Oak Ave & Broadway | Reduces downtown parking demand |

## 🚶‍♂️ Quick Guides by Neighborhood

### If You Live in 80304 (Near This Guide's Featured Commute)
1. Start on Oak Avenue — enjoy the quiet, tree-lined residential street
2. Use 19th Street — the main north-south corridor with bus service
3. End on Canyon Boulevard — Boulder's best separated bike path
4. **Time:** ~48 min walking, ~16 min biking

### If You Live in 80302 (Downtown)
1. You're already there! 1800 Broadway is in the heart of downtown
2. Walk to your office in ~5–10 minutes
3. Use B-Cycle for errands and after-work activities
4. **Time:** Already at your destination!

### If You Live in 80301 (East Hill / CU Campus)
1. ~1–2 km to 1800 Broadway — the shortest commute
2. Mostly flat, very walkable
3. **Time:** ~10–15 min walking, ~5 min biking

### If You Live in 80303 (South Boulder / Table Mesa)
1. ~3–5 km — moderate terrain
2. Uses Arapahoe Avenue or 28th Street corridors
3. **Time:** ~35–50 min walking, ~15–20 min biking

### If You Live in 80305 (Gunbarrel / North)
1. ~5–7 km — the longest commute in this guide
2. More road sharing, fewer bike lanes
3. **Time:** ~65–85 min walking, ~25–35 min biking
