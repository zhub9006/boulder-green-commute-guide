# 🌱 Sustainability Tips for Boulder Commuters

## How 4 km of Walking/Biking Makes a Difference

### Daily Impact
- **CO₂ saved per day**: ~0.8 kg (avoiding a car trip)
- **CO₂ saved per year** (250 workdays): ~200 kg
- **Calories burned** (walking): ~250 kcal per one-way trip
- **Calories burned** (biking): ~200 kcal per one-way trip
- **Money saved**: ~$500-1000/year (gas + parking + maintenance)

### Annual Impact (If 4 Boulder Residents Made This Same Switch)
- ~800 kg CO₂ saved annually
- ~1000+ kcal burned per person per workday
- Reduced traffic on 19th Street — a major north-south artery
- Less demand for downtown parking

## General Tips for Boulder Green Commuting

### 🚶 Walking Tips
1. **Start small**: Even 1-2 days per week makes a difference
2. **Invest in good shoes**: A comfortable pair of walking shoes makes 4 km feel easy
3. **Layer up**: Boulder weather changes fast — keep a light jacket at the office
4. **Use Canyon Boulevard**: The path along Boulder Creek makes the most beautiful segment — enjoy it!
5. **Walk with others**: Find a walking buddy from your office for motivation

### 🚲 Biking Tips
1. **Use a bike rack**: Downtown Boulder has bike racks — use them!
2. **Learn the route**: 19th Street is the main segment — practice it on a weekend
3. **Check infrastructure**: Boulder has invested in bike lanes/sharrows on many streets
4. **Storage**: Many Boulder offices have bike storage — ask your HR department
5. **All-weather readiness**: Fenders and a rain jacket make 4 km doable in any Colorado weather

### 🚌 Combined Approaches
1. **Walk + Bus**: The 4 km walk gets you to Downtown Boulder Station (230m from office) — on bad weather days, take the bus for that last 230m
2. **Bike + Bus**: Many RTD buses have bike racks — combine biking to a bus stop for longer distances
3. **Park & Walk**: If you must drive, park at a friend's place or a free lot and walk the last 2-3 km

### 🏙️ Advocacy Tips
1. **Share your data**: Use this repository's methodology to document YOUR commute
2. **Talk to your employer**: Encourage them to provide bike storage, shower facilities, and walking/biking incentives
3. **Support Boulder's transportation initiatives**: The City of Boulder has extensive plans for pedestrian and bike infrastructure
4. **Community matters**: Share this guide with colleagues and neighbors

## Boulder's Transportation Advantage

Boulder is uniquely positioned for green commuting:
- 🚲 **Bike infrastructure**: Extensive network of bike paths, including the Boulder Creek Path
- 🚶 **Walkable downtown**: One of the most walkable downtowns in Colorado
- 🚌 **RTD buses**: Downtown Boulder Station is right near many offices
- 🏔️ **Flat terrain**: Most of Boulder is relatively flat — biking is accessible to most people
- 🌲 **Beautiful routes**: Canyon Boulevard, the Creek Path, and tree-lined streets make walking/cycling pleasant
