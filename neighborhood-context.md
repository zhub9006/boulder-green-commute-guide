# 🏙️ Neighborhood Context

Your commute connects two distinctive Boulder neighborhoods, each with its own character, amenities, and transit connections.

---

## 🌳 Northern End: 45 Oak Avenue (ZIP 80304, North Boulder)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Character** | Quiet, residential, tree-lined street near CU Boulder campus area |
| **Housing** | Primarily single-family homes and small apartment buildings |
| **Walk Score** | ~72 (Somewhat Walkable) |
| **Bike Score** | ~80 (Very Bikeable) |

### Nearby Amenities
- **Oak Creek Trail** — Popular walking/biking path along the creek, connects to CU Boulder campus
- **CU Boulder Campus** — University of Colorado, less than 1 mi north
- **Boulder Waldorf Preschool** — Local childcare
- **Neighborhood park** — Open green space and playground nearby
- **RTD Bus stops** — Route 201/202 on Arapahoe Avenue (0.3–0.5 mi away)

### What You'll Pass Early On
- Oak Avenue is a calm residential street with mature trees — a pleasant start to any commute
- As you head south on 19th Street, you'll pass through CU-influenced neighborhoods with a mix of students and families

### Transit Connections

- **B-Cycle** — Easy bike-sharing for one-way trips or bad weather backup
- **Downtown Boulder Park & Ride** — Not directly adjacent but within 0.5 mi

## 🗺️ The In-Between: Route Through Two Neighborhoods

Your commute flows through three distinct zones:

### Zone 1: Residential North (Mile 0–1.5)
- Oak Avenue → 19th Street
- Quiet, low-traffic, tree-lined
- Mix of students and established families near CU campus
- Sidewalks on both sides of the street

### Zone 2: Transition (Mile 1.5–2.5)
- 20th Street → Pearl Street
- Transitioning from residential to mixed-use
- 20th Street is a major east-west connector with moderate traffic
- Pearl Street bridges the gap to downtown — more foot traffic, more amenities

### Zone 3: Downtown Core (Mile 2.5–4.0)
- 18th Street → Canyon Boulevard → Broadway
- Urban, higher foot traffic, dedicated bike lanes on Canyon Blvd
- Well-maintained sidewalks, frequent crosswalks
- Pulse of downtown Boulder
