### Walking Route Highlights

- 🏙️ **Downtown Boulder Experience:** The route passes through Pearl Street — Boulder's vibrant downtown core with shops, cafés, and restaurants
- ☀️ **Well-Lit & Populated:** The corridor is well-lit and populated during daytime hours
- 🌊 **Boulder Creek Connection:** The route connects to the Boulder Creek Path near Canyon Boulevard
- 🎓 **Campus Proximity:** Passes near University of Colorado Boulder and downtown campus areas
- 🔔 **Amenities Along the Way:** Multiple water fountains, benches, and crosswalks

### Walking Tips for This Route

- 💧 **Stay Hydrated:** Boulder's elevation (5,430 ft / 1,655 m) increases dehydration risk even on short walks
- 🧴 **Sunscreen Required:** UV exposure is significantly stronger at high altitude
- 👟 **Wear Supportive Shoes:** Sidewalks are mostly even, but some older sections near downtown can be uneven
- 🕐 **Best Timing:** Daytime is ideal; the route is well-populated and well-lit in commercial areas
- 🌧️ **Rain Gear:** Boulder rain is usually brief but can be intense — carry a packable rain jacket

### Biking Route Highlights

- 🛡️ **Bike Infrastructure:** 19th Street and Canyon Boulevard have dedicated bike lanes
- 🏙️ **Downtown Passage:** Bike through Pearl Street's vibrant core with bike parking available
- 🌲 **Canyon Boulevard:** A major bike corridor in Boulder with wide shoulders and bike lanes
- ❄️ **Year-Round Usable:** Boulder's mild winters make this route viable most of the year
- 🔋 **E-Bike Friendly:** The route is flat enough that e-bike assistance makes it effortless

### Biking Tips for This Route

- 🪖 **Helmet Required:** Colorado law requires helmets for riders under 18; recommended for all
- 💡 **Use Lights:** Front and rear lights are essential — especially critical at Boulder's high altitude where dusk comes early
- 📱 **Use Bike Lanes:** 19th Street and Canyon Boulevard have the best bike infrastructure on this route
- 🧥 **Dress in Layers:** Boulder weather can shift 20°F+ in a single day at altitude
- 🧊 **Winter Riding:** Studded tires and fenders recommended November–March; check [Boulder Bike Paths Map](https://bouldercolorado.gov/healthrecreation/bike-paths) for closures
- 🅿️ **Bike Parking:** Bike racks are available along Pearl Street and near 1800 Broadway

## Alternative & Multi-Modal Options

### Bike + Bus (RTD)
If the weather is bad, combine biking with RTD bus service:
- The **101, 102, and SB1** routes serve the Broadway corridor
- Use RTD's bike rack on the front of buses for part of the journey
- Check schedules at [rtd-denver.com](https://rtd-denver.com/)

### B-Cycle Bike Share
- Boulder's bike-share program has stations near downtown
- Great for one-way trips if you don't need a bike at both ends
- Visit [bouldercolorado.gov/bicycle](https://bouldercolorado.gov/healthrecreation/bike-paths) for station locations

### Walk + Bus
- If walking the full distance isn't feasible, combine with RTD bus service
- Reduced walking distance + public transit = still car-free!
