# 🌱 Sustainable Commuting Tips for Boulder

## Quick Wins
- 🚶 Walk for trips under 1 km
- 🚴 Bike for trips 1-5 km
- 🚌 Use RTD for longer distances
- 🚗 Only drive when truly necessary

## Reduce, Reuse, Rethink

### The 5-Minute Rule
If your trip is under 15 minutes by car, try walking or biking instead.

### Trip Chaining
Combine multiple errands into one active commute — hit the coffee shop, post office, and grocery store on your way.

### Weather Hierarchy
1. ☀️ Sunny and 50-75°F: Walk or bike always
2. 🌧️ Rain/Drizzle: Bike with rain gear (Boulder has microclimates)
3. ❄️ Snow/Ice: Bike if you have studs; otherwise bus or car-share
4. 🌡️ Extremes (heat/cold): RTD bus with bike rack

### Boulder-Specific Eco Hacks
- **Bike to work bonus:** Many Boulder employers offer bike-to-work incentives
- **Shower facilities:** Downtown Boulder has several gyms and community centers with day passes
- **Change of clothes:** Keep a kit at your desk — no need for locker rooms
- **Coffee stop:** Railroad Jack's or The Leaf on the way — make it part of your routine

## Community Actions
- **Boulder Transportation Master Plan:** Get involved in city planning for better bike/ped infrastructure
- **Bicycle Boulder:** Advocacy group for cyclist rights and safety
- **Walking Summit:** Annual event celebrating walking culture
- **Neighborhood associations:** Push for safer streets in your area

## Factoring Climate Impact

| Mode | CO₂ per trip | CO₂ per year (200 work days) |
|------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Walking | 0 kg | 0 kg |
| Biking | 0 kg | 0 kg |
| Bus (RTD) | ~0.3 kg | ~60 kg |
| Car (solo) | ~1.4 kg | ~280 kg |
| Car (carpool) | ~0.7 kg | ~140 kg |

*Based on Boulder average vehicle emissions of 0.7 kg CO₂/mile and EPA data*

## Bonus: Calories & Fitness

| Mode | Calories per trip | Annual burn (200 work days) |
|------|-------------------|-----------------------------|
| Walking | ~220 | ~44,000 cal |
| Biking | ~110 | ~22,000 cal |

That's walking off ~12 pounds or biking off ~6 pounds of fat per year — just from commuting!

### BMI-Friendly Target
Walking 4 km daily = ~14,600 steps (well above the 10,000 daily goal)
This single habit contributes significantly to meeting the CDC's 150 min/week exercise recommendation.
