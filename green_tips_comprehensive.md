# 🌿 Green Commuting Tips & Seasonal Guide

## Your Route at a Glance
**45 Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway | 4.03 km | ~15m elevation gain**

---

## 🌸 Spring (March–May)
- **Weather:** Unpredictable — 40–65°F, afternoon thunderstorms possible
- **Tip:** Layer clothing; carry a lightweight rain jacket
- **Watch for:** Afternoon storms (typically 2–4 PM); muddy trails on Canyon Blvd
- **Best riding days:** Mornings before 2 PM
- **Gear:** Waterproof fenders, light jacket, panniers for wet gear

## ☀️ Summer (June–August)
- **Weather:** Dry heat 85–95°F; intense UV at 5,430 ft elevation
- **Hydration:** Drink 0.5L per 30 minutes of riding; start hydrated
- **Sun protection:** SPF 30+ sunscreen, UV-blocking sunglasses, lightweight long sleeves
- **Tip:** Ride early morning (6–9 AM) or evening (5–7 PM) to avoid peak heat
- **Gear:** Light-colored clothing, cooling towel, electrolyte packets

## 🍂 Fall (September–November)
- **Weather:** Best riding season — 55–75°F, low humidity, stunning aspens
- **Tip:** Golden aspen display along 19th Street corridor — beautiful commute!
- **Watch for:** Afternoon wind gusts through Canyon Blvd corridor
- **Gear:** Light layers (temps drop quickly after 4 PM), wind layer

## ❄️ Winter (December–February)
- **Weather:** Snow/ice possible Nov–Mar; temps 15–40°F; short days
- **⚠️ #1 hazard:** Black ice on bridges and shaded sections (especially 19th St & Canyon Blvd)
- **Essential gear:**
  - Studded tires for ice traction
  - Front + rear lights (headlight + reflector — legally required in Boulder after dark)
  - Insulated gloves, warm hat/ear warmers
  - Waterproof jacket and pants
- **Route tips:**
  - Canyon Boulevard is better maintained and has separated bike/pedestrian path
  - Use RTD bus if conditions are unsafe (routes 1, 3, 7, 10 on 19th St)
  - E-bike with torque sensor handles snow/ice better than traditional bikes
- **Tip:** Allow extra time; ride at reduced speed; avoid sudden braking

## 💚 Sustainable Commuting Habits

1. Reusable everything — Water bottle, coffee mug, shopping bags
2. Combine trips — Bike to lunch at Pearl Street cafés instead of driving
3. Support local — Grab coffee at Brewing Market or Boulder Baked on route
4. Bike for groceries — Use panniers instead of driving to King Soopers on 28th St
5. Maintain your bike — Regular cleaning & lubrication extends life, reduces waste
6. Choose eco-friendly transportation — B-Cycle e-bikes at stations near your route
7. Share the road — Boulder has a strong cycling community; be courteous & visible
