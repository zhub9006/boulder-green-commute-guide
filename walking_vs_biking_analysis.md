## 🚲 Biking — Full Analysis

### Route Flow
```
45 Oak Avenue (40.0449°N) → South on Oak Ave (96.6m) — share with pedestrians
  ↓ Right onto 19th St (2,156.5m) — use painted bike lane ⚠️ DOORING RISK
  ↓ Left onto 20th St (106.1m) → Quick connector
  ↓ South on 20th St (565.6m) — road, no bike lane; residential low traffic
  → Right onto Pearl St (216.2m) — DISMOUNT ZONE! Walk bike through mall
  → Left onto 18th St (248.8m) — downtown intersection
  → Right onto Canyon Blvd (636.6m) — SEPARATED BIKE LANE ⭐ SAFEST!
  ↓ 
1800 Broadway (40.0163°N) ✅
```

### Biking Advantages
- **3× faster** — 16 min vs 48 min walking
- **Zero emissions** — 100% CO₂-free
- **Health benefits** — ~200 kcal burned, cardio exercise, low-impact on joints
- **Canyon Boulevard** — 636.6 m of dedicated separated bike lane (16% of route) — Boulder's best bike infrastructure
- **Mostly flat** — only ~15 m elevation gain, no steep hills
- **B-Cycle available** — ~$3.50/trip if you don't own a bike
- **Time savings add up** — 32 min saved each way = 64 min/day = 5.3 hours/week

### Biking Challenges
- **19th Street** — 2,156.5 m on a busy arterial (54% of route); dooring risk in painted bike lane; RTD buses frequent
- **Pearl Street Mall** — Must dismount and walk bike through ~216 m pedestrian zone
- **Downtown intersections** — 18th Street has vehicle turning conflicts
- **Theft concern** — Moderate bike theft in downtown Boulder; always use a U-lock
- **Winter riding** — Ice/snow risk Nov–Mar on 19th Street; studded tires recommended
- **Speed cameras** — Boulder has speed enforcement on major arterials

## 💡 Pro Tips

### For Walkers 🚶‍♂️
1. **Start early** — Winter daylight is limited; plan morning departures accordingly
2. **Layer up** — Boulder mornings can be 15-20°F colder than afternoons at 5,430 ft
3. **Use Canyon Blvd** — The final 637 m on a dedicated separated path is a wonderful way to end your commute
4. **Weekend exploration** — Try walking the route on weekends to explore Pearl Street Mall and downtown cafés

### For Cyclists 🚲
1. **19th Street caution** — Dooring risk is real; ride confidently in the bike lane, not near parked cars
2. **Dismount on Pearl** — It's the law and common sense; walk your bike through the mall zone
3. **Canyon Blvd is your reward** — The 636.6 m separated bike lane is the best infrastructure on the route
4. **B-Cycle e-bikes** — Consider an e-bike for the 19th Street segment; makes the arterial easier
5. **Winter gear** — Fenders, lights, and reflective clothing are essential Nov–Mar
