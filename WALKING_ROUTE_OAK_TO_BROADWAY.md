## Walking Safety Tips

1. **Pedestrian right-of-way:** Colorado law gives pedestrians the right-of-way at marked crosswalks and intersections
2. **Visibility:** Wear bright or reflective clothing, especially during early morning or evening walks
3. **Crosswalks:** Use designated crosswalks at intersections — don't jaywalk
4. **Traffic awareness:** Even on residential streets, stay alert for turning vehicles
5. **Buddy system:** Consider joining a walking group or finding a walking buddy for added safety

## Weather & Seasonal Considerations

### Spring/Fall (Ideal)
- Temperatures typically 50–75°F — perfect walking weather
- Bring a light jacket or sweater for temperature changes
- Wildflower season (April–May) makes for a beautiful commute

### Summer
- Temperatures can reach 85–95°F — bring plenty of water
- Afternoon thunderstorms (June–August) — plan your route to seek shelter if needed
- Early morning walks are cooler and less crowded

### Winter
- Temperatures 20–45°F — dress in layers
- Snow and ice possible — wear boots with good traction
- Shorter daylight hours — wear reflective gear and use a headlamp
