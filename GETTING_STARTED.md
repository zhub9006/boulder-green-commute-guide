# Getting Started with This Guide

This guide demonstrates how to analyze walking and biking commute options for any Boulder, CO route using OpenStreetMap data.

## Example: My Commute
- **From**: 45 Oak Avenue, Boulder, CO 80304
- **To**: 1800 Broadway, Boulder, CO 80302
- **Distance**: 4.0 km / 2.5 miles

## Quick Results

| Mode | Time | Cost | CO₂ Emissions | Calories Burned |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 🚶 Walking | ~50–60 min | Free | 0 g | ~300–400 kcal |
| 🚴 Biking | ~10–15 min | Free | 0 g | ~100–150 kcal |

## How This Analysis Was Created

1. **Geocode** home and work addresses via OpenStreetMap Nominatim
2. **Analyze commute** using OSM router for both `foot` and `bike` modes at 8:00 AM departure
3. **Get route directions** — turn-by-turn for each mode
4. **Explore neighborhood** — amenities, walkability, transit access
5. **Calculate impact** — CO₂ savings, health benefits vs. driving

## Data Sources
- OpenStreetMap (routing, geocoding, amenities)
- Boulder, CO pedestrian/bike infrastructure maps

## Adapting for Your Own Route

1. Identify your home and work addresses
2. Geocode them to get coordinates
3. Run commute analysis for walking + biking
4. Get detailed turn-by-turn directions
5. Profile the neighborhoods along your route
6. Calculate annual environmental and health impact
7. Share your findings!

## Tips for Success
- Best analysis time: 7–9 AM (rush hour gives realistic conditions)
- Check seasonal conditions before committing to a year-round mode
- Test the route on weekends before commuting during workdays
- Combine modes: walk one way, bike the other, for variety
