## Bike Infrastructure Notes

### Bike Lanes & Routes
- **Canyon Boulevard:** Has a separated multi-use path — the safest and most pleasant section of the commute
- **20th Street:** Painted bike lanes in some sections; shared road elsewhere
- **Downtown (Pearl/18th):** No dedicated bike lanes; use caution and dismount in crowded areas

### Bike Parking at 1800 Broadway
- **Rack location:** Check for RTD bike racks near the Broadway transit center
- **Indoor options:** Building may have bike parking inside — ask facilities management
- **Nearby:** Several business bike racks along Broadway between 18th and 20th

### Recommended Bike Types
- **Cruiser:** Great for flat Boulder terrain and the scenic Canyon Boulevard
- **Hybrid/Commuter:** Efficient for the moderate distance with optional cargo capacity
- **E-bike:** Perfect if you want an extra boost on the 19th Street stretch

## Safety Tips for Cyclists

1. **Wear a helmet** — Always, no exceptions
2. **Use lights** — Front white, rear red. Essential for early morning and evening rides
3. **Signal turns** — Use hand signals at every intersection
4. **Watch for pedestrians** — Especially on Pearl Street and downtown sections
5. **Stay visible** — Wear bright/reflective clothing, especially in low-light conditions
6. **Lock up properly** — Use a U-lock through the frame and front wheel

## Elevation Profile

Flat! Boulder is famously bike-friendly with minimal elevation changes. Expect a gentle descent from Oak Avenue (~5,430 ft) to Broadway (~5,340 ft).
