## Turn-by-Turn Directions

### Segment 1: Oak Avenue (97 m, ~16 seconds)
📍 Start: 45 Oak Avenue, Boulder, CO 80304

- Exit your home and head south on Oak Avenue
- This short residential stretch connects you to 19th Street
- Use the sidewalk if the street is too narrow for comfortable cycling
- Proceed to the intersection with 19th Street

**⚠️ Note:** Oak Avenue is a quiet residential street — easy to mount your bike here. Keep in mind the speed limit is typically 25 mph in Boulder residential zones.

---

### Segment 2: 19th Street (2.16 km, ~3.5 minutes)
📍 Major artery with bike infrastructure

- Turn left onto 19th Street heading south
- **This is your longest segment and the most important for bike infrastructure**
- 19th Street has a designated multi-use corridor
- You'll pass several intersections — use hand signals
- **Bike lane coverage** is available on this stretch

**💡 Tip:** This is the most efficient cycling stretch. Maintain a steady pace of 15-20 km/h. The street has minimal elevation change and good sight lines.

**🏪 Nearby:** Boulder Cycle Sport (bike shop on Broadway), cafes near 19th Street

---

### Segment 3: 20th Street (672 m, ~1.3 minutes)
📍 Transition toward downtown

- Continue south on 20th Street
- **Bike infrastructure remains consistent** through this area
- Watch for turning vehicles at intersections
- You're entering a more densely built area

---

### Segment 4: 20th Street continued (566 m, ~1.1 minutes)
📍 Approaching the Pearl Street area

- Continue on 20th Street
- **Traffic volume increases** as you get closer to downtown
- Use the bike lane or ride with traffic if no lane is present
- Signal your turns clearly

---

### Segment 5: Pearl Street (216 m, ~26 seconds)
📍 Downtown Boulder — bike-friendly street

- Turn left onto Pearl Street heading east
- Pearl Street is Boulder's historic core — **wide, pedestrian-friendly sidewalks**
- **Cyclists are welcome but should be mindful of pedestrians**
- The pace here should be slow and careful

**💡 Tip:** Pearl Street is often busy with pedestrians, especially on weekends. Dismount and walk your bike if the crowd is dense.

**🏪 Nearby:** Ruzo Coffee, Lucky's Bakehouse Cafe, Boulder Cycle Sport

---

### Segment 6: 18th Street (249 m, ~37 seconds)
📍 Short connector to Broadway

- Turn right onto 18th Street heading south
- Brief connector with good visibility
- **Use the bike lane** if available; otherwise ride near the right edge
- This connects you to the main bike corridor

---

### Segment 7: Canyon Boulevard (637 m, ~1 minute)
📍 Major bike corridor — final stretch

- Continue on Canyon Boulevard heading south
- **Canyon Boulevard is one of Boulder's premier bike routes**
- Dedicated bike lanes are clearly marked
- Smooth pavement, wide lanes
- You'll arrive at 1800 Broadway at the end of this segment

**🏁 Destination:** 1800 Broadway, Boulder, CO 80302

## Safety Tips

1. **Use hand signals** at every turn — even if no cars are present
2. **Make eye contact** with drivers at intersections
3. **Stay predictable** — ride in a straight line, signal your intentions
4. **Be visible** — wear bright/reflective clothing, use lights
5. **Watch for pedestrians** — especially on Pearl Street and downtown
6. **Avoid distractions** — no phone use while riding
7. **Yield to pedestrians** — it's the law and the considerate thing to do
