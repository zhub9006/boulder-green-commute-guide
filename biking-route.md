# 🚲 Biking Route: 45 Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway, Boulder

## Route Overview

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Total Distance** | 4.03 km (2.5 miles) |
| **Estimated Time** | ~14 minutes |
| **Calories Burned** | ~120 kcal |
| **Elevation Change** | ~30 m (100 ft) gain |
| **Bike Infrastructure** | Mixed (bike lanes, low-traffic streets) |
| **Max Speed Limit** | 30 mph (on Canyon Blvd) |

## Step-by-Step Directions

### Leg 1: Oak Avenue (96.6 m, ~10 sec)
Start from 45 Oak Avenue, heading south on **Oak Avenue**. This short residential segment connects you to 19th Street. Traffic is light — residential speed limits and no through-traffic issues.

### Leg 2: 19th Street (2,156.5 m, ~3 min)
Turn right onto **19th Street**. This is the core of your bike commute. 19th Street has a **dedicated bike lane** in the northbound direction, making this the safest and most comfortable cycling segment. You'll ride past residential areas, small businesses, and cross several side streets. This is your cruising segment — maintain a steady pace.

### Leg 3: 20th St. (106.1 m, ~5 sec)
Turn left onto **20th Street**. This is a tight but safe intersection. Cross with care — it's a two-way street with moderate traffic.

### Leg 4: 20th Street (565.6 m, ~1 min)
Continue on **20th Street** heading east. This is a residential street with bike-opportunities (shared road hints). Traffic flows at 20–25 mph. Stay in the right portion of the lane.

### Leg 5: Pearl Street (216.2 m, ~5 sec)
Turn onto **Pearl Street**. Pearl Street has a **dedicated bike lane** running alongside the sidewalks. This is a high-comfort segment — the bike lane is physically separated from motor traffic in many sections.

### Leg 6: 18th Street (248.8 m, ~7 sec)
Turn left on **18th Street**. This is a short segment before your final turn. Watch for pedestrians — Pearl Street is a popular downtown access point.

### Leg 7: Canyon Boulevard (636.6 m, ~1 min)
Turn onto **Canyon Boulevard** heading west. This is the most caution-intensive segment. Canyon Boulevard is a major arterials with **30 mph speed limit** and higher traffic volumes. There is a **bike lane** but it narrows near the Broadway intersection. Stay visible, use hand signals, and maintain a steady line.

### Leg 8: Arrival at 1800 Broadway
Continue on Canyon Boulevard to **1800 Broadway**. Bike racks are typically available near the Broadway commercial corridor. Lock up securely!

## Route Characteristics

- **Bike lane coverage**: ~60% of route (19th St bike lane, Pearl St bike lane, Canyon Blvd bike lane)
- **Traffic exposure**: Moderate — Canyon Boulevard is the highest-traffic segment; other streets are low-volume
- **Safety rating**: High — well-marked bike infrastructure with clear sightlines
- **Hill factor**: Nearly flat — the 30 m elevation gain is manageable for all fitness levels
- **Intersection quality**: 8 intersections total, all signalized with bike-friendly timing

## Best Time to Bike

- **Morning (7–8:30 AM)**: Light traffic on 19th Street, cool temps
- **Afternoon (4–5:30 PM)**: Afternoon commute window, good visibility
- **Avoid**: Peak commute (8:30–9 AM, 4:30–5:30 PM) on Canyon Blvd during summer months

## Weather & Altitude Tips

- **Altitude**: At 5,430 ft, you'll feel the thinner air — take it easier on climbs and hydrate well
- **Wind**: Boulder is known for sudden wind gusts, especially along Canyon Boulevard — be prepared
- **Sun**: UV is intense at altitude — wear sunscreen and a helmet with visor
- **Rain**: Summer thunderstorms can roll in fast — carry a waterproof jacket or use B-cycle for backup
- **Snow/Ice**: November–March mornings can be icy — check tire pressure and use wider tires if possible

## B-cycle Bikeshare Option

If you don't own a bike, use **Boulder B-cycle**:
- **Cost**: $12/month for unlimited 60-min rides
- **Stations**: Multiple stations along Pearl Street and Broadway
- **Reserve bikes** via the B-cycle app to guarantee availability during peak hours
- **E-bikes available** at select stations — great for the Canyon Boulevard climb
