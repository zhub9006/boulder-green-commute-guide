## Step-by-Step Directions

### Leg 1: Oak Avenue (96.6 m, ~14 sec)
Start from 45 Oak Avenue, heading south on **Oak Avenue**. This short residential segment connects you to 19th Street. Traffic is light — residential speed limits and no through-traffic issues. Absolutely no bike infrastructure needed here.

### Leg 2: 19th Street (2,156.5 m, ~3 min 29 sec)
Turn right onto **19th Street**. This is the core of your bike commute. 19th Street has a **dedicated bike lane** in the northbound direction, making this the safest and most comfortable cycling segment. You'll ride past residential areas, small businesses, and cross several side streets. This is your cruising segment — maintain a steady pace. The gentle downhill makes this effortless.

### Leg 3: 20th St. (106.1 m, ~12 sec)
Turn left onto **20th Street**. This is a tight but safe intersection. Cross with care — it's a two-way street with moderate traffic. The bike lane ends here; merge into the travel lane for the short connector.

### Leg 4: 20th Street (565.6 m, ~1 min 9 sec)
Continue on **20th Street** heading east. This is a residential street with bike-opportunities (shared road markings). Traffic flows at 20–25 mph. Stay in the right portion of the lane. University Hill area — watch for student cyclists and pedestrians.

### Leg 5: Pearl Street (216.2 m, ~26 sec)
Turn onto **Pearl Street**. Pearl Street has a **dedicated bike lane** running alongside the sidewalks. This is a high-comfort segment — the bike lane is physically separated from motor traffic in many sections. Note: You must dismount in the Pearl Street Mall pedestrian section.

### Leg 6: 18th Street (248.8 m, ~34 sec)
Turn left on **18th Street**. This is a short segment before your final turn. Watch for pedestrians — Pearl Street is a popular downtown access point. The bike lane continues through here.

### Leg 7: Canyon Boulevard (636.6 m, ~1 min 20 sec)
Turn onto **Canyon Boulevard** heading west. This is the most caution-intensive segment. Canyon Boulevard is a major arterial with **30 mph speed limit** and higher traffic volumes. There is a **bike lane** but it narrows near the Broadway intersection. Stay visible, use hand signals, and maintain a steady line. This is the "go slow" section — make sure you're seen by drivers.

### Leg 8: Arrival at 1800 Broadway
Continue on Canyon Boulevard to **1800 Broadway**. Bike racks are typically available near the Broadway commercial corridor. Lock up securely! 🎉

## Best Time to Bike

- **Morning (7–9 AM)**: Light traffic on 19th St; moderate on Canyon Blvd. Enjoy the downhill!
- **Midday (11 AM–1 PM)**: Best visibility, steady traffic on all streets. Pearl St mall section requires dismounting.
- **Evening (4–6 PM)**: Rush hour — heavier traffic on Canyon Blvd. Allow 20+ min. Stay visible!
- **Winter**: Check bike lane snow clearance. Ride slower on Canyon Blvd (ice risk near Broadway).

## Safety Tips

1. **Be visible**: Front and rear lights required by Colorado law after dark
2. **Helmet**: Recommended, required for B-cycle users
3. **Hand signals**: Required by law for turns and stops
4. **Canyon Blvd caution**: Narrow bike lane near Broadway — stay single-file
5. **Pearl Street Mall**: Dismount in the pedestrian mall section (11th–13th Streets)
6. **Locking**: Use a high-quality U-lock + cable; bike racks available on Broadway
